Supply Chain Material Composition Notes
========================================

Last Updated: February 2026

These estimates reflect the approximate percentage of product cost attributable to raw materials and commodities, based on supplier invoices and BOM analysis.

Category: Watches
- Precious metals (gold, silver, platinum): ~5% of product cost
- Stainless steel and alloys: ~15%
- Electronics components: ~10%
- Notes: Higher-end watches use more gold in casings and bands

Category: Electronics
- Rare earth elements: ~2% of product cost
- Copper and wiring: ~3%
- Silicon and semiconductors: ~8%
- Notes: Broad category with varying material profiles

Category: Cameras
- Optical glass: ~6%
- Rare earth elements (for lenses): ~3%
- Aluminum housing: ~4%
- Notes: Specialty glass is the primary cost driver

Category: Audio (including Headphones and Speakers)
- Copper (voice coils, wiring): ~4%
- Rare earth magnets: ~3%
- Plastics and polymers: ~5%
- Notes: Speaker drivers require neodymium magnets

Category: TV & Home Theater
- Rare earth phosphors: ~2%
- Copper wiring: ~3%
- Glass panels: ~8%
- Notes: Display panels are the dominant cost factor

Category: Home Appliances
- Steel and iron: ~10%
- Copper (motors, wiring): ~5%
- Aluminum: ~3%
- Notes: Motor-driven appliances have higher metal content

Summary of Total Material Cost Percentages:
- Watches: 30%
- Electronics: 13%
- Cameras: 13%
- Audio (Headphones): 12%
- Audio (Speakers): 12%
- TV & Home Theater: 13%
- Home Appliances: 18%
