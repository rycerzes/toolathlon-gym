This guide explains how to select optimal high-speed trains for business trips in China.

Train Selection Criteria:
Prefer trains with the G prefix (Gaotie, meaning high-speed). These are the fastest and most reliable option for intercity business travel.

For outbound travel, select the train that arrives earliest at the destination while still having a reasonable morning departure time. This maximizes the amount of productive time at the destination before a meeting.

For return travel, select a train that departs after any scheduled meetings conclude. A morning departure from the destination allows arrival back before the end of the business day.

Seat Class Guidance:
Second-class seats (二等座) are standard and comfortable for trips under 6 hours.
First-class seats (一等座) offer more space and are suitable for longer journeys.
Business-class seats (商务座) provide premium comfort and are typically reserved for executive travel.

Document Format:
Each travel plan document should include the train number, origin station, departure time, destination station, arrival time, total travel duration, seat class selected, and ticket price. A booking summary should list the costs for each leg and the grand total.

Important Notes:
Always verify seat availability before finalizing the booking.
Build in at least 30 minutes buffer before any meetings for transit from the station.
Second-class ticket price for Beijing South to Shanghai Hongqiao is typically 553 CNY.
