We need to restock some low-inventory products urgently. Please check our product catalog and find all products where the stock quantity is five units or fewer.

Create an Excel file called Low_Stock_Reorder.xlsx with a sheet called Reorder List containing columns for Product_Name (truncated to the first fifty characters), Current_Stock, Price rounded to two decimal places, and Reorder_Priority. The Reorder_Priority should be set to Urgent if the stock is zero, High if the stock is one or two units, and Normal if the stock is three to five units. Sort the rows by Current_Stock ascending and then by Price descending within each stock level. Add a second sheet called Summary with two columns, Metric and Value, containing the following rows: Total_Low_Stock_Items, Urgent_Count for products with zero stock, High_Priority_Count for products with one or two units, and Normal_Priority_Count for products with three to five units.

Schedule a reorder review meeting in the calendar on March 15 2026 from 2pm to 3pm with the title Urgent Stock Reorder Review. Also schedule a follow-up meeting on March 22 2026 at the same time titled Stock Replenishment Follow-up.

Send an email to procurement@company.com with subject Low Stock Alert - Immediate Reorder Required that lists all products currently at zero stock and requests urgent action.

Create a Word document called Reorder_Report.docx with a heading Low Stock Reorder Report and a table listing all low-stock products with their names, stock levels, prices, and reorder priorities.
