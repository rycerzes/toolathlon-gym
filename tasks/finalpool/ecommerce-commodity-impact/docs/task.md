I run an online store and I want to understand how commodity and market price trends relate to our sales performance. I need you to pull our sales data, retrieve some commodity and market pricing data, keep track of your key findings as you go, and compile everything into a structured Excel report.

First, look at the online shop's completed order data to determine sales revenue broken down by product category. Only consider orders with a status of "completed" when computing revenue figures. For each completed order, examine the individual line items to determine which product category each item belongs to, and calculate the revenue as the sum of quantity multiplied by unit price for each item. Aggregate this information to get the total revenue, the number of distinct completed orders, and the average order value for each category.

Second, retrieve commodity and market price data for three symbols over the past six months. Specifically, look up the gold futures contract (GC=F), Exxon Mobil Corporation (XOM) as a proxy for energy sector performance, and the Dow Jones Industrial Average (^DJI) as an overall market benchmark. For each symbol, determine the closing price approximately six months ago (around September 5, 2025, using the closest available trading day on or before that date) and the most recent closing price (March 5, 2026, or the closest available trading day on or before that date). Compute the percentage change as (latest minus earlier) divided by earlier, multiplied by 100.

As you work through the analysis, store key findings along the way. Record entities such as category names with their revenue figures and commodity symbols with their price changes, and create relations linking them. This is important for maintaining an audit trail of your analytical process.

Finally, compile all findings into a single Excel file named Commodity_Impact_Report.xlsx saved in your workspace. The file must contain exactly three sheets.

The first sheet must be named "Category Sales" with columns Category, Total_Revenue, Order_Count, and Avg_Order_Value. Include one row per product category from completed orders. Sort rows by Total_Revenue in descending order. Round Avg_Order_Value to two decimal places (Avg_Order_Value equals Total_Revenue divided by Order_Count).

The second sheet must be named "Commodity Trends" with columns Symbol, Name, Price_6M_Ago, Latest_Price, and Change_Pct. Include exactly three rows for GC=F (named "Gold Futures"), XOM (named "Exxon Mobil"), and ^DJI (named "Dow Jones"). Round Change_Pct to two decimal places.

The third sheet must be named "Analysis Summary" with two columns named Metric and Value. Include the following rows in this exact order. Top_Category is the category with the highest total revenue. Top_Category_Revenue is that category's total revenue rounded to two decimal places. Bottom_Category is the category with the lowest total revenue. Bottom_Category_Revenue is that category's total revenue rounded to two decimal places. Total_Store_Revenue is the sum of all category revenues rounded to two decimal places. Gold_6M_Change_Pct is the gold futures percentage change rounded to two decimal places. Energy_6M_Change_Pct is the XOM percentage change rounded to two decimal places. Market_6M_Change_Pct is the Dow Jones percentage change rounded to two decimal places. Market_Trend should be "Bullish" if the Dow Jones Change_Pct is greater than 5, "Bearish" if it is less than negative 5, or "Neutral" otherwise.

Save the Excel file and ensure your memory entries are preserved in the workspace.
