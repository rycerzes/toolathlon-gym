You are a pricing strategist for an online electronics and accessories retailer. The company sells products across categories including Audio, Cameras, Electronics, Home Appliances, TV and Home Theater, and Watches. Leadership is concerned about how commodity price movements, particularly gold futures, might affect product costs and pricing strategy.

Start by retrieving the product catalog from the store. Get all products and note their names, prices, and categories. Group the products by category and calculate the average price, minimum price, maximum price, and total product count for each category.

Next, get the gold futures price data (ticker symbol GC=F) from the financial data service. Retrieve the most recent 20 trading days of closing prices. Also retrieve stock price data for Amazon (AMZN) and Google (GOOGL) as e-commerce sector comparators.

Write and run a Python pricing impact analysis script. The script should calculate the 20-day moving average for gold prices and determine the trend direction (whether the latest price is above or below the moving average). For each product category, estimate the commodity sensitivity based on these rules: Watches have 40 percent gold sensitivity, Electronics have 15 percent, Audio and Cameras have 10 percent, TV and Home Theater has 5 percent, and Home Appliances has 8 percent. Calculate the estimated material cost impact as the average category price multiplied by the sensitivity percentage. Also compute the correlation between gold price changes and e-commerce stock price changes over the 20-day period.

Create a cloud spreadsheet titled "Commodity Pricing Dashboard" with three sheets. The first sheet "Product_Categories" should have columns Category, Product_Count, Avg_Price, Min_Price, Max_Price, and Commodity_Sensitivity_Pct. The second sheet "Gold_Price_Trend" should have columns Date, Gold_Close, Moving_Avg_20d, and Trend (Up if close is above the moving average, Down otherwise) for the 20 most recent trading days. The third sheet "Pricing_Impact" should have columns Category, Avg_Price, Sensitivity_Pct, Estimated_Material_Cost, Current_Gold_Price, and Recommended_Action (where categories with sensitivity above 20 percent should say "Review Pricing" and others should say "Monitor").

Create a Word document called Pricing_Strategy_Report.docx in the workspace. The report should have a title "Commodity Price Impact Analysis", followed by an executive summary discussing the current gold price trend and its implications. Include a section on category-level exposure analysis showing which categories are most sensitive to commodity prices. Include a section on the gold price trend with the 20-day moving average analysis. End with a recommendations section suggesting specific pricing actions for high-sensitivity categories like Watches.

Your workspace contains pricing_assumptions.json with the sensitivity percentages and margin_targets.md with target margin information by category.
