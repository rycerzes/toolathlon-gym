The Learning and Development director wants to build a targeted training program based on departmental skill gaps identified from employee performance and education data. A training catalog API is available at http://localhost:30219/api/courses.json that provides a list of available training courses, each with course name, skill area, duration in hours, cost per attendee, and maximum capacity per session.

Start by fetching the complete training catalog from the API endpoint. Then query the company data warehouse to analyze employee education levels and average performance ratings by department. The warehouse contains approximately 50,000 employee records across seven departments: R&D, Finance, Operations, Sales, HR, Support, and Engineering. Each employee has an education level (High School, Diploma, Bachelor's, Master's, or PhD) and a performance rating from 1 to 5.

Identify skill gap departments by finding the three departments with the lowest average performance ratings. For these departments, also calculate the percentage of employees with only a High School or Diploma education level, which indicates potential training needs.

Create a Notion database titled "Training Program 2026" to serve as the training program tracker. For each of the three lowest-performing departments, create a page entry with properties for Department (title), Avg_Performance (number), Training_Priority (select: High, Medium, or Low based on ranking), Assigned_Course (text with the most relevant course name from the catalog), and Estimated_Cost (number calculated as cost per attendee multiplied by the number of employees in that department who have High School or Diploma education).

Create an Excel workbook called "Training_Budget_Plan.xlsx" in the workspace with three sheets. The first sheet should be called "Department Analysis" with columns for Department, Total_Employees, Avg_Performance, High_School_Count, Diploma_Count, Bachelors_Count, Masters_Count, PhD_Count, and Low_Education_Pct (percentage of High School plus Diploma employees). The second sheet should be called "Training Catalog" listing all courses from the API with columns Course_Name, Skill_Area, Duration_Hours, Cost_Per_Attendee, and Max_Capacity. The third sheet should be called "Budget Allocation" with columns for Department, Assigned_Course, Trainees (count of High School plus Diploma employees), Sessions_Needed (calculated from trainees divided by max capacity, rounded up), Cost_Per_Session, Total_Cost, and Training_Priority.

Schedule three Google Calendar events for the training sessions, one per priority department. Use dates May 4, May 11, and May 18, 2026, each from 9:00 AM to 5:00 PM. Each event summary should be "[Department] Training: [Course Name]" and the description should include the number of trainees and estimated cost.

Send an email from ld-director@company.com to department-managers@company.com with the subject "2026 Training Program - Department Assignments". The body should summarize which departments were identified as priority, which courses were assigned to each, and the total training budget.

Save all output files to the workspace directory.
