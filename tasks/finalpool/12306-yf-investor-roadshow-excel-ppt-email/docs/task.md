You are a financial analyst at an investment firm preparing an investor roadshow. You will travel from Beijing to two cities, Shanghai and Guangzhou, on March 10, 2026, to present quarterly results to institutional investors. Your task is to combine travel planning with financial analysis, producing a comprehensive Excel workbook, a professional investor presentation, and sending meeting coordination emails.

Start by checking the current date and retrieving the station codes for Beijing, Shanghai, and Guangzhou. Query available high-speed rail services from Beijing South Station to Shanghai Hongqiao Station on March 10, 2026. Also query services from Beijing South Station to Guangzhou South Station on March 10, 2026. For each route, note the train number, departure time, arrival time, and journey duration. Identify the best option for each route, specifically the G11 service for Shanghai and the G105 service for Guangzhou. Retrieve the seat pricing for the business class seats on both trains.

Next, retrieve financial data for the company being presented. Look up the stock information for the company with ticker symbol AAPL. Fetch its historical price data for the past 30 days to assess recent performance trends. Retrieve the income statement financial data to identify revenue and net income figures. Obtain analyst recommendation data to summarize the current consensus view. Fetch the latest five news headlines for context. Also retrieve comparative stock information and recent price history for the company with ticker MSFT to enable a peer comparison discussion. Compute or note key metrics including approximate price change over the past 30 days for both companies.

Create an Excel workbook named Roadshow_Analysis.xlsx. Add a sheet named Travel_Plan with columns City, Train_No, Departure, Arrival, Duration_Hours, Class, Ticket_Price_CNY, and Meeting_Date. Add two rows, one for the Shanghai leg using G11 and one for the Guangzhou leg using G105, both on March 10, 2026, both in business class. Add a sheet named Stock_Summary with columns Company, Ticker, Current_Price, Change_30D_Pct, Market_Cap_B, Revenue_B, EPS, and Recommendation. Add rows for both AAPL and MSFT using the most recent available figures from the financial data system. Add a sheet named Financial_Highlights with rows covering at least two years of AAPL revenue and net income data, using columns Metric, Year, and Value_B.

Create a presentation file named Investor_Roadshow.pptx containing at least five slides. The first slide should be a title slide with a heading that references the investor roadshow and the year 2026. The second slide should present the travel itinerary with the two city visits laid out as a table or structured text. The third slide should highlight the key financial metrics for the company being presented, covering revenue growth, net income, and earnings per share. The fourth slide should describe the 30-day stock performance comparison between the two companies. The fifth slide should summarize the analyst consensus and key recommendations.

Send an email to investors@fundmanager.com informing them about the roadshow schedule, mentioning that both the Excel workbook and presentation have been prepared. Send a separate email to shanghai_partners@finance.com with the specific details of the Shanghai meeting on March 10, including the expected arrival time and meeting logistics.

The file roadshow_config.json in your workspace specifies the primary ticker AAPL, the comparison ticker MSFT, the roadshow date of March 10, 2026, the target cities, the departure city of Beijing, and the preferred seat class of business class. The file presentation_guide.md explains the expected slide structure, how to present financial highlights, how to organize the stock comparison, and how to structure the Excel sheets.
