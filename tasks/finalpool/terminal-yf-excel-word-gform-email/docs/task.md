The investment committee needs a quarterly portfolio performance review for the company's equity holdings. The portfolio consists of five stocks: Amazon (AMZN), Alphabet (GOOGL), JPMorgan Chase (JPM), Johnson and Johnson (JNJ), and ExxonMobil (XOM). Review the portfolio guidelines and risk parameters in the workspace before starting.

Pull the current stock information and historical price data for all five holdings from the financial data service. Run financial analysis scripts that calculate the total return percentage for each stock (based on the earliest and most recent closing prices in the available history), the annualized volatility (standard deviation of daily returns multiplied by the square root of 252), and a simple Sharpe ratio using the risk-free rate specified in the risk parameters file.

Create an Excel file called Quarterly_Portfolio_Review.xlsx with three sheets. The first sheet "Performance_Summary" should have columns Symbol, Name, Sector, Current_Price, Total_Return_Pct (rounded to 1 decimal), and Annualized_Volatility_Pct (rounded to 1 decimal), sorted by Symbol. The second sheet "Risk_Metrics" should have columns Symbol, Sharpe_Ratio (rounded to 2 decimals), Max_Drawdown_Pct (rounded to 1 decimal, calculated as the largest peak-to-trough decline), sorted by Symbol. The third sheet "Portfolio_Summary" should have columns Metric and Value with rows: Total_Holdings (number 5), Best_Performer (symbol with highest total return), Worst_Performer (symbol with lowest total return), Highest_Volatility (symbol), Lowest_Volatility (symbol), Avg_Total_Return_Pct (average of all total returns rounded to 1 decimal).

Write a Word document called Portfolio_Review_Report.docx with an executive summary covering overall portfolio performance, a section analyzing individual stock performance and identifying the best and worst performers, a risk analysis section discussing volatility and Sharpe ratios, and concluding recommendations for portfolio rebalancing.

Create a online survey form titled "Q1 2025 Investor Feedback Survey" with at least four questions: a rating question about overall portfolio satisfaction on a scale of 1 to 5, a multiple choice question about risk tolerance preference, a short answer question for additional comments, and a question about preferred communication frequency.

Finally, email the quarterly review to investment_committee@company.com with the subject "Q1 2025 Portfolio Performance Review" and include a brief summary of the key performance metrics in the email body.
