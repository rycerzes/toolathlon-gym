The company wellness team is preparing healthy eating educational materials for an upcoming employee wellness week. Your job is to browse the recipe database available in your workspace, select exactly 5 dishes from at least 4 different recipe categories, and then evaluate each dish's nutritional profile using the nutrition reference guide file called nutrition_guide.md located in your workspace.

For each selected recipe, identify the main ingredients from the recipe and look up their nutritional information in the guide. Estimate the total calories for the dish by summing up the per-100g calorie values of the key ingredients, determine the overall protein level as the highest protein level among the main ingredients, and determine the overall fiber level as the highest fiber level among the main ingredients. Rate difficulty on a scale of Easy, Medium, or Hard based on the number of cooking steps in the recipe where 5 or fewer steps is Easy, 6 to 10 steps is Medium, and more than 10 steps is Hard.

Create an Excel workbook called Nutrition_Comparison.xlsx in the workspace. It should contain two sheets. The first sheet must be named Recipe Overview and include columns Recipe_Name, Category, Difficulty, Estimated_Calories, Protein_Level, and Fiber_Level. Recipe_Name values must be in English and should be reasonable translations of the original recipe names. The second sheet must be named Recommendation and include columns Rank, Recipe_Name, Health_Score, and Reason. Calculate Health_Score using the formula described in the nutrition guide. Rank the recipes from highest to lowest Health_Score and include at least the top 3 recipes in the Recommendation sheet. The Reason column should contain a brief English explanation of why that recipe is recommended.

Next, create a PowerPoint presentation called Healthy_Eating_Guide.pptx in the workspace. The first slide should be a title slide with a title that includes the words Healthy Eating Guide. After the title slide, include one slide for each recommended recipe from the Recommendation sheet showing the recipe name as the slide title and its category, estimated calories, health score, and reason for recommendation in the slide body. End the presentation with a summary slide that lists all recommended recipe names and a brief overall healthy eating tip.

The presentation should have at least 7 slides total including the title slide, the individual recipe slides, and the summary slide.
