Health Score Calculation Method

To calculate the Health Score for a recipe, use the following formula:

Health_Score = (Protein_Level_Points + Fiber_Level_Points) * 10 - Estimated_Calories / 100

Where the point values are:
High = 3 points
Medium = 2 points
Low = 1 point

Protein_Level is the highest protein level among the main ingredients of the recipe.
Fiber_Level is the highest fiber level among the main ingredients of the recipe.
Estimated_Calories is the sum of calories per 100g for each main ingredient used in the recipe (use 3 to 5 key ingredients, excluding minor seasonings like salt, soy sauce, and vinegar).

For example, if a recipe has Protein_Level High (3 points) and Fiber_Level Medium (2 points) with Estimated_Calories of 350, the Health_Score would be (3 + 2) * 10 - 350 / 100 = 50 - 3.5 = 46.5.

A higher Health_Score indicates a healthier recipe. Recipes with a Health_Score above 40 are considered excellent, 30 to 40 is good, and below 30 is moderate.
