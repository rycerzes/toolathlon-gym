Literature Review Research Scope
=================================

Topic: Large Language Models and Transformer Architectures

Selection Criteria:
- Papers must be about transformer architectures, large language models, or instruction tuning
- Publication years: 2017 through 2023
- Minimum citation threshold: 5,000 citations preferred (exceptions for highly relevant 2023 papers)
- At least 6 papers must be included in the review

Key Research Questions:
1. How have transformer architectures evolved since the original "Attention Is All You Need" paper?
2. What training techniques (pretraining, fine-tuning, RLHF) have enabled modern LLMs?
3. What are the key scaling laws and their implications?
4. How do open-source models compare to proprietary ones?

Search Terms:
- "transformer language model"
- "large language models"
- "GPT BERT LLM"
- "attention mechanism NLP"

Expected Output:
The literature review document should cover papers in order of citation count (highest first),
and include a synthesis section identifying the major themes and directions in the field.

Presentation:
The team presentation is scheduled for 14 days after task launch. All team members should
read at least 2 papers before the presentation date.
