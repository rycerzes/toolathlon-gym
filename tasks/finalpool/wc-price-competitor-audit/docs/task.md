You are a pricing analyst for an online retail store that sells electronics, audio equipment, cameras, watches, home appliances, and related accessories. Your main competitor, TechRival, publishes their current product price list online at http://localhost:30200/index.html and you need to conduct a thorough price comparison audit.

Start by reviewing the pricing policy document and analysis notes in your workspace to understand the competitive threshold and comparison methodology. The pricing policy specifies that a product is considered competitively priced if the difference between our price and the competitor price is within plus or minus 10 percent of the competitor price. Products where our price exceeds the competitor price by more than 10 percent should be flagged as overpriced, and products where our price is more than 10 percent below the competitor price should be flagged as underpriced.

Next, retrieve the complete product catalog from your online store, focusing on all published products and their regular prices. You will also need to fetch the competitor price list from the URL mentioned above. The competitor page contains a table with product names, categories, prices, and customer ratings for about 20 products, though not all of them will match products in your store. Some products on the competitor page are items you do not carry.

Carefully match products between your store and the competitor by comparing product names. Only include products that appear in both catalogs. For each matched product, calculate the price difference as your regular price minus the competitor price, and the percentage difference as that difference divided by the competitor price times 100. Based on the percentage difference, assign a status of Overpriced if the difference exceeds positive 10 percent, Underpriced if the difference is below negative 10 percent, or Competitive if the difference falls within plus or minus 10 percent.

Create an Excel file called Price_Comparison.xlsx in your workspace with two sheets. The first sheet should be named "Price Match" and contain the following columns: Product_Name, Our_Price, Competitor_Price, Difference, Difference_Pct, and Status. Include one row for every matched product. The second sheet should be named "Summary" and contain two columns labeled Metric and Value, with rows for Total_Products_Compared, Overpriced_Count, Underpriced_Count, Competitive_Count, and Avg_Price_Difference_Pct which is the average of all the Difference_Pct values.

Also create a Word document called Competitive_Analysis.docx in your workspace. This document should have a heading titled Competitive Price Analysis Report, followed by a summary section listing the total products compared and the counts of overpriced, underpriced, and competitively priced products along with the average price difference percentage. Include a section titled Overpriced Products Requiring Review with a table showing each overpriced product name, our price, competitor price, and the difference percentage. Finally add a recommendations section with guidance on reviewing overpriced products in accordance with the company pricing policy.

When you have completed all tasks, call claim_done.
