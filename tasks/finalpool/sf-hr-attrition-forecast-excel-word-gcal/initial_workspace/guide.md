Attrition Risk Analysis Guide

This guide explains how to perform the employee attrition risk assessment using the scoring methodology defined in the Retention Policy PDF.

Calculating Salary Growth

For each employee who has salary change records, compute the percentage change for each event as (NEW_SALARY - OLD_SALARY) / OLD_SALARY * 100. Then compute the average of all percentage changes for that employee. Employees with no salary history records receive no score contribution from the salary growth factor (treated as 0 points).

Risk Score Computation

Each employee is scored on four factors as described in the Retention Policy. The factors are additive. After computing the total score, classify the employee into a risk level: Low (0-2), Medium (3-4), or High (5+).

Department Median Salary

For the Experience Factor, you need each department's median salary. Calculate this across all employees in the department to determine whether an experienced employee is below median.

Output Format

The Excel file should contain three sheets:
- Risk Assessment: grouped by Department and Risk_Level with aggregate metrics
- Department Summary: one row per department with risk counts and budget
- Top Risk Factors: the single most impactful factor per department

The Word report should contain narrative sections covering the analysis findings and recommended interventions.
