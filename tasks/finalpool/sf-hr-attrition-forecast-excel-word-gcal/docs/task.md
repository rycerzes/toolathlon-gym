Our HR leadership team needs a comprehensive attrition risk analysis to identify employees who may be at risk of leaving the organization. There is a PDF document called Retention_Policy.pdf in the workspace that describes the risk scoring framework we use, along with a guide.md file that explains the methodology in more detail. Please read both of these documents carefully before starting the analysis.

We need you to pull employee data and salary history records from our company data warehouse. For each employee, calculate a risk score based on four factors described in the retention policy: salary growth stagnation, low job satisfaction, low performance ratings, and whether experienced employees are being underpaid relative to their department peers. Use terminal to run any analysis scripts you need for the data processing.

Once you have computed the risk scores, create an Excel file named Attrition_Risk_Analysis.xlsx in the workspace with three sheets. The first sheet should be named "Risk Assessment" and should contain one row for each combination of Department and Risk_Level, sorted first by Department alphabetically and then by Risk_Level alphabetically within each department. The columns should be Department, Risk_Level, Employee_Count, Avg_Satisfaction, Avg_Performance, and Avg_Salary_Growth_Pct, with averages rounded to one decimal place. The second sheet should be named "Department Summary" with one row per department sorted alphabetically, containing columns Department, Total_Employees, High_Risk_Count, Medium_Risk_Count, Low_Risk_Count, High_Risk_Pct (percentage of high risk employees rounded to one decimal), and Total_Intervention_Budget (calculated as high risk count times 5000 plus medium risk count times 2000). The third sheet should be named "Top Risk Factors" with one row per department, containing columns Department, Top_Factor (the factor name that contributes the most total risk score points across all employees in that department), Factor_Score (the average per-employee contribution of that factor rounded to one decimal), and Recommended_Action (a brief description of the recommended intervention for that factor).

Next, create a Word document named Intervention_Report.docx in the workspace. This report should include the following sections: an Executive Summary that provides an overview of the total number of employees in each risk category and the total intervention budget, a Department-by-Department Analysis section with a subsection for each of the seven departments showing their risk breakdown and top contributing factor, a Budget Overview section with a table showing per-department budget allocation, a Recommended Actions section with organization-wide recommendations, and a Timeline section outlining next steps.

Finally, schedule seven one-hour department review meetings on the calendar, one for each department. These meetings should take place during the week of March 16 through March 20 2026 during business hours. Each meeting should be titled with the department name followed by "Attrition Risk Review" and include a brief description mentioning the department's high risk count and recommended intervention budget. Space the meetings out so there are no conflicts.
