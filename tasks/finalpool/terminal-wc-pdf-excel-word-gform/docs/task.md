You are a quality assurance analyst conducting a product quality audit for an electronics e-commerce platform. Your objective is to analyze product reviews and refund patterns, identify quality concerns, produce audit reports, and create a supplier feedback survey.

Start by reading the Quality_Standards.pdf in your workspace. It defines the acceptable defect rate thresholds, minimum average rating requirements, and review volume benchmarks for each product category. Also read the audit_scope.json file, which specifies the product categories to include in the audit and the rating thresholds that trigger quality flags.

From the e-commerce platform, retrieve product review data across all categories. You need the review ratings, product associations, and category breakdowns. Also pull order data including order statuses, totals, and any associated refund information. The platform has six product categories, and you should analyze all of them.

Using command-line tools, write and execute a Python script called quality_audit.py in your workspace. The script should perform the following analysis. For each product category, calculate the number of products, total number of reviews, and the average rating. Compute the percentage of reviews that are below three stars out of five. For order and refund data, calculate the count and total amount for each order status, with special attention to refunded and cancelled orders. Generate a quality score for each category on a scale of zero to one hundred, calculated as the average rating divided by five multiplied by one hundred, then penalized by subtracting twice the percentage of low reviews. Assign risk levels based on these scores: High risk if the score is below 70, Medium if between 70 and 85, and Low if above 85.

Create an Excel workbook called Product_Quality_Audit.xlsx in your workspace with four sheets. The first sheet named Review_Summary should contain columns for category name, product count, average rating, total review count, and percentage of reviews below three stars. Include one row for each of the six product categories. The second sheet named Refund_Analysis should contain columns for order status, order count, total amount, and percentage of total orders. Include one row for each order status found in the data. The third sheet named Quality_Scorecard should contain columns for category name, quality score, and risk level. Include one row per category. The fourth sheet named Survey_Questions should contain columns for question number, question text, and question type. List at least five questions that would be suitable for a supplier quality feedback survey, covering topics like defect rates, response times, quality improvement plans, packaging standards, and overall satisfaction.

Create a Word document called Quality_Audit_Report.docx in your workspace. The document should include a title, an executive summary paragraph describing the overall findings, a section on review analysis by category highlighting any categories with concerning quality scores, a section on refund patterns noting the proportion of refunded orders, and a recommendations section with at least three specific actions for quality improvement.

Finally, create a feedback survey form titled "Supplier Quality Feedback" with a description mentioning product quality assessment. The form should contain at least five questions matching the ones listed in your Excel survey questions sheet. Include a mix of question types such as rating scales, multiple choice, and open-ended text questions.
