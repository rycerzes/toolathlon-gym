Our engineering team wants a comprehensive performance analysis of a popular technology channel from our video database. The channel publishes short-form videos about programming and software topics. Your job is to process the raw video data, compute quarterly statistics, and produce polished deliverables for our quarterly report.

Step one: retrieve all videos from our video database for the channel named Fireship. Export the raw data to a file called fireship_raw.csv in your workspace with the following columns in this exact order: video_id, title, published_date, duration_sec, view_count, like_count. The published_date should be formatted as YYYY-MM-DD and duration_sec is the duration in seconds as an integer.

Step two: write and run a Python analysis script called analysis.py in your workspace. The script should read fireship_raw.csv, group the videos by calendar quarter (e.g. 2024-Q1, 2024-Q2, 2025-Q1, etc.), and for each quarter compute: the total number of videos published (Video_Count), the sum of all view counts (Total_Views), and the average engagement rate defined as total likes divided by total views multiplied by 100, rounded to 3 decimal places (Avg_Engagement_Rate). Write the results to a file called quarterly_stats.json in your workspace. The JSON file should be a list of objects each with keys Quarter, Video_Count, Total_Views, Avg_Engagement_Rate, sorted chronologically by Quarter.

Step three: create an Excel workbook called Fireship_Analytics.xlsx in your workspace with three sheets. The first sheet is called Raw_Data and contains all available Fireship videos with columns video_id, title, published_date, duration_sec, view_count, like_count. The second sheet is called Quarterly_Stats with columns Quarter, Video_Count, Total_Views, Avg_Engagement_Rate. The rows in Quarterly_Stats must be sorted chronologically. The third sheet is called Summary and must contain the following key-value pairs as rows: Total_Videos (total number of videos), Total_Views (sum of all view counts across all videos), Best_Quarter (the quarter with the highest Total_Views), Worst_Quarter (the quarter with the lowest Total_Views), Overall_Avg_Engagement (the overall average engagement rate across all videos, rounded to 3 decimal places). The Summary sheet should have two columns named Metric and Value.

Step four: write a Word document called Fireship_Analytics_Report.docx in your workspace with the following four sections. The first section is titled Executive Summary and contains a paragraph summarizing the channel size, total views, and overall average engagement. The second section is titled Quarterly Performance and contains a brief narrative describing which quarter had the highest views and which had the lowest. The third section is titled Key Observations and notes at least two interesting observations about the data. The fourth section is titled Appendix and states that the data was sourced from the internal video database and covers all available published videos.
