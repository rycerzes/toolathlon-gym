You are an HR analyst tasked with designing a mentorship program by pairing senior high-performers with junior employees.

Query the HR employee database to identify suitable mentor and mentee candidates. Senior mentors should be employees with at least 10 years of experience and a performance rating of 4 or above. Select the top 10 such candidates ordered by performance rating descending, then years of experience descending. Junior mentees should be employees with at most 2 years of experience and a performance rating of 3 or above. Select the top 10 such candidates ordered by performance rating descending.

Create an Excel file named Mentorship_Pairs.xlsx in the workspace with two sheets:

Sheet named Pairs with columns: Mentor_Name, Mentor_Department, Mentor_Rating, Mentee_Name, Mentee_Department, Mentee_Experience. Pair mentors and mentees sequentially (first mentor with first mentee, second with second, etc.). There should be 10 rows of pairs.

Sheet named Program_Summary with two columns: Metric and Value. Include these rows: Total_Pairs (value: 10), Avg_Mentor_Rating (average performance rating of the selected mentors, rounded to 2 decimal places), Avg_Mentee_Experience (average years of experience of the selected mentees, rounded to 2 decimal places).

Schedule a Google Calendar event titled Mentorship Kickoff Meeting exactly 7 days after the launch time. The event should last 1 hour and include a description mentioning the mentorship program.

Send an email from hr@company.example.com to program@hr.example.com with the subject Mentorship Program Launch. The body should summarize the program including total pairs and any relevant details.

The Mentorship_Guidelines.pdf file in your workspace contains the mentorship program guidelines for reference.
