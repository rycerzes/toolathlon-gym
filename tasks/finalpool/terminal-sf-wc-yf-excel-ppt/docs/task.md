The CEO wants a unified cross-platform business intelligence dashboard that combines enterprise sales data, e-commerce performance, and stock market conditions into a single executive briefing. There are two files in the workspace to get started: dashboard_spec.json which defines the KPIs we track along with their target values and weights, and industry_benchmarks.csv which contains sector-level benchmarks for comparison. There is also a last_quarter_summary.xlsx file that has the previous quarter numbers we will use for year-over-year comparison.

Start by reading dashboard_spec.json to understand what KPIs are needed and how they should be calculated. Then read industry_benchmarks.csv for the benchmark figures. Next, pull the enterprise sales data from the company data warehouse. We need total revenue broken down by customer segment, which should be Enterprise, SMB, Consumer, and Government. Also pull the monthly revenue trend for the most recent 12 months from the orders table, along with the top 5 products by revenue.

From the e-commerce platform, pull revenue by product category, plus customer acquisition metrics including total customer count, total order count, average order value, and total e-commerce revenue.

For market context, look up three symbols: the Dow Jones Industrial Average for overall market health, Amazon for e-commerce sector benchmark, and JPMorgan Chase for financial sector health. Get the latest price and any year-to-date change information available.

Write a Python script called merge_data.py that combines all three data streams into a single unified_metrics.json file. Then write a second script called health_score.py that computes a composite business health score using these weights: enterprise revenue performance gets 40%, e-commerce growth gets 30%, and market position gets 30%. The enterprise score should be calculated as actual total enterprise revenue divided by the target of 3200000, capped at 100. The e-commerce score is based on total e-commerce revenue divided by target 70000, capped at 100. The market score is 80 if the Dow Jones latest close is above 40000, otherwise 50. Save the result to business_health.json with fields enterprise_score, ecommerce_score, market_score, and composite_score, all rounded to 1 decimal.

Create an Excel workbook called Executive_Dashboard.xlsx with four sheets. The first sheet "Enterprise_Sales" should have columns Segment, Revenue, Customer_Count, Avg_Deal_Size, and Order_Count sorted alphabetically by Segment. Revenue should be rounded to 2 decimal places. Avg_Deal_Size is Revenue divided by Order_Count, rounded to 2 decimals. The second sheet "Ecommerce_Performance" should have columns Category, Revenue, Product_Count, and Avg_Order_Value. List each product category with its total revenue from orders rounded to 2 decimals, count of distinct products sold in that category, and the overall average order value across all orders rounded to 2 decimals. Sort by Revenue descending. The third sheet "Market_Context" should have columns Symbol, Latest_Price, and Asset_Name. Include rows for the three symbols mentioned above. Latest_Price is the most recent closing price. Asset_Name should be "Dow Jones Industrial Average" for the index, "Amazon.com Inc" for AMZN, and "JPMorgan Chase & Co" for JPM. The fourth sheet "Unified_Dashboard" should have columns KPI_Name, Current_Value, Target, Status, and Source. Include these rows: Enterprise_Total_Revenue with the sum of all segment revenues as Current_Value and 3200000 as Target, Ecommerce_Total_Revenue with total e-commerce revenue and 70000 as target, Market_Index with the Dow Jones latest close and 45000 as target, Top_Segment with the name of the highest-revenue segment as Current_Value and no target, Top_Ecommerce_Category with the highest-revenue e-commerce category name as Current_Value and no target. Status should be "On Track" if Current_Value meets or exceeds Target, "Below Target" otherwise, or "N/A" for rows without a target. Source should be "Enterprise DW", "E-Commerce Platform", "Financial Markets", "Enterprise DW", and "E-Commerce Platform" respectively.

Create a PowerPoint presentation called Executive_Briefing.pptx with eight slides. Slide 1 is the title slide with "Cross-Platform Business Intelligence Dashboard" as the title and "Executive Briefing" as subtitle. Slide 2 is "Business Health Score" showing the composite score and its three components. Slide 3 is "Enterprise Sales Overview" summarizing revenue by segment. Slide 4 is "E-Commerce Performance" covering category revenue breakdown. Slide 5 is "Market Context" with the three market indicators. Slide 6 is "Year-over-Year Comparison" comparing current metrics to last quarter values from the summary file. Slide 7 is "Risk Factors" identifying any KPIs that are below target. Slide 8 is "Strategic Priorities" with recommended actions based on the analysis.

Finally, write a Python script called generate_alerts.py that reads unified_metrics.json and checks each KPI against its target from dashboard_spec.json. Any KPI where the current value is below its target should generate an alert entry with fields kpi_name, current_value, target, gap_percentage rounded to 1 decimal. Save the alerts to alerts.json as a list.
