You are a business development manager for TechStore Online, an e-commerce company selling consumer electronics and accessories. Your task is to plan a product roadshow in Shanghai and Guangzhou to meet regional distributors, selecting the right products to demonstrate based on store sales data, arranging train travel from Beijing, building a comprehensive roadshow plan spreadsheet, documenting the plan in the company knowledge base, and notifying all key contacts.

Start by analyzing the online store data. Retrieve the top ten products by sales volume from the product sales report. For each of the top five products, get the full product details including name, price, category, and stock status. Then retrieve the customer list and examine the billing location information to understand regional customer distribution. Also pull the orders report to understand regional revenue patterns. Based on this analysis, identify Shanghai and Guangzhou as the two primary markets for the roadshow.

Next, plan the train travel. Look up the station codes for Beijing, Shanghai, and Guangzhou using the train booking system. Query available high-speed trains from Beijing South station to Shanghai Hongqiao station on March 10, 2026. Note the options available including G1 departing at 09:00 and G11 departing at 07:00. Also query trains from Beijing South to Guangzhou South on March 10, 2026, identifying G105 which departs at 08:00 and arrives at 15:48. Select G11 as the preferred train to Shanghai because it arrives at 12:31 allowing time to prepare for afternoon meetings, and G105 as the Guangzhou option.

Create an Excel workbook named Roadshow_Plan.xlsx in your workspace. It must contain four sheets. The first sheet should be named Products and contain columns: Product_ID, Name, Category, Price_USD, Stock_Status, and Monthly_Sales_Est. Populate it with the five products selected for demonstration. The second sheet should be named Customer_Regions and contain columns: Region, Customer_Count, Revenue_Share_Pct, and Priority. Add rows for the key regions identified in the customer data analysis, marking Shanghai and Guangzhou as Priority 1. The third sheet should be named Travel_Itinerary and contain columns: Date, Train_No, From, To, Depart, Arrive, Duration_Hours, and Purpose. Add a row for the G11 journey to Shanghai on March 10 and a row for the G105 journey to Guangzhou. The fourth sheet should be named Roadshow_Schedule and contain columns: City, Date, Venue, Meeting_Type, Key_Contacts, and Status. Add at least four rows covering the Shanghai meeting on March 10 and the Guangzhou meeting on March 11.

Create a page in the company knowledge base titled "Product Roadshow 2026 - Shanghai and Guangzhou". Add a section highlighting the five demonstration products with key selling points for each. Add a travel itinerary section showing both train journeys with departure and arrival details. Add a checklist section called "Follow-up Actions" listing items such as sending product catalogs to distributors, collecting distributor feedback forms, and scheduling follow-up calls.

Send an email to shanghai_dist@partner.com with subject "Roadshow Meeting Confirmation - Shanghai March 10" providing full details of the planned meeting time, venue, and products to be demonstrated. Send a second email to guangzhou_dist@partner.com with subject "Roadshow Meeting Confirmation - Guangzhou March 11" with the Guangzhou meeting details and a list of products.
