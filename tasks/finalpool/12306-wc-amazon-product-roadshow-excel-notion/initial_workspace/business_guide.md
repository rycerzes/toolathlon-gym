Product Roadshow Planning Guide

This guide explains the process for planning a regional product roadshow, from product selection through travel planning to meeting coordination.

Product Selection for Roadshow Demonstrations

Analyze your online store data to identify the top five products for demonstration. Prioritize products by total sales volume, not just unit price. For each selected product, note the category, current price, stock availability, and estimated monthly sales. Products with high sales volume in the target region should be prioritized. For an electronics and accessories store, typical high-performing categories include wireless audio, mobile accessories, computing peripherals, and smart home devices.

Regional Customer Analysis

Group existing customer orders by billing state or city to understand regional revenue contribution. Express each region's contribution as a percentage of total revenue. Regions with more than 20 percent revenue share should be classified as Priority 1 and are the primary roadshow targets. Regions between 10 and 20 percent are Priority 2. Use this analysis to justify the choice of Shanghai and Guangzhou as the roadshow cities.

Travel Planning Rules

When booking train travel, select trains that arrive at least one hour before the first scheduled meeting. For the Beijing to Shanghai route, G11 departing at 07:00 and arriving at 12:31 provides adequate preparation time for a 14:00 meeting. For the Beijing to Guangzhou route, G105 departing at 08:00 and arriving at 15:48 is suitable for meetings the following morning. Always book first class for business travel. The Beijing to Shanghai first class ticket price is 933 yuan. The Beijing to Guangzhou first class ticket price is 1383 yuan.

Roadshow Meeting Schedule Template

Each city visit should follow this schedule. 09:00 to 10:00 is company and product portfolio overview. 10:00 to 12:00 is live product demonstration covering all five selected products. 12:00 to 13:00 is business lunch with distributor management. 13:00 to 15:00 is commercial negotiation and terms discussion. 15:00 to 16:00 is facility tour and logistics review.

Post-Roadshow Follow-up

Within 48 hours after each meeting, send a follow-up email with meeting notes, product specification sheets, and proposed distributor terms. Add follow-up call reminders to the shared calendar two weeks after the roadshow.
