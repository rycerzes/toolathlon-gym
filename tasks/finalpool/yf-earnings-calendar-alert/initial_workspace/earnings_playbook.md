# Earnings Season Playbook

## Pre-Earnings Strategy

Before each earnings announcement, the team should review the following:

1. Compare expected EPS against the company's historical average to gauge whether analysts are being optimistic or conservative.
2. If the expected EPS is significantly above the historical average, consider trimming the position to reduce risk.
3. If expected EPS is in line with or below the historical average, maintain the current position size.

## Alert Protocol

For each stock with upcoming earnings, send a summary alert to the investment team at least two weeks before the earnings date. The alert should include:

- The stock symbol and expected earnings date
- The consensus EPS estimate
- Whether the estimate is above, below, or in line with historical performance
- Any notable financial trends from recent quarters

## Definitions

"In Line" means the expected EPS is within 5 percent of the historical average EPS.
"Above" means expected EPS exceeds the historical average by more than 5 percent.
"Below" means expected EPS is below the historical average by more than 5 percent.
