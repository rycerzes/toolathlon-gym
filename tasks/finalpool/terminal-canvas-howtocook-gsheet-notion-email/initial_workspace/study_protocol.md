# Nutrition-Academic Performance Correlation Study

## Study ID: SA-2026-NUT-001

## Principal Investigator
Dr. Sarah Chen, Department of Student Affairs

## IRB Approval
IRB Protocol #2026-0142, approved January 15, 2026

## Study Objective
To determine whether targeted nutritional interventions correlate with improved academic engagement and performance among undergraduate students enrolled in Environmental Economics & Ethics courses.

## Methodology

### Phase 1: Data Collection
- Extract enrollment and submission data from courses 13 and 14 (Environmental Economics & Ethics, Fall 2013 and Fall 2014 cohorts)
- Categorize students into engagement tiers based on late submission patterns
- Tier definitions:
  - **High Engagement**: 0% late submission rate
  - **Medium Engagement**: >0% and <=25% late submission rate
  - **Low Engagement**: >25% late submission rate

### Phase 2: Intervention Design
- Design meal plan interventions based on engagement tier
- Control group (High engagement): No meal intervention
- Partial intervention (Medium engagement): Breakfast recommendations only
- Full intervention (Low engagement): Full breakfast and lunch meal plans
- Use campus cooking database for healthy recipe selection

### Phase 3: Pilot Implementation
- Target start date: April 1, 2026
- Duration: 12 weeks
- Track academic performance changes across tiers

## Budget Constraints
- Refer to campus_dining_budget.json for per-student cost limits
- Total pilot budget: $50,000

## Data Privacy
- All student data must be de-identified in reports
- Use student IDs only, no names
- Aggregate statistics for groups of 10 or more students only

## Deliverables
1. Student engagement analysis with tier breakdown
2. Meal plan recommendations per tier
3. Cloud spreadsheet for ongoing tracking
4. Knowledge base entries for program management
5. Email notifications to stakeholders
6. Summary report
