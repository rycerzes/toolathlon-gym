The HR team needs a comprehensive compensation market benchmark analysis to understand how our internal salaries compare against industry standards. The market salary benchmark data is available as a JSON file at http://localhost:30208/api/salary_benchmarks.json and should be fetched to obtain the current market rates for each department and role. You will also need to query our internal employee data warehouse to get the actual salary statistics for every department and role combination, including the average salary and employee count.

Start by reading the compensation policy document in the workspace to understand the company's target compensation positioning and the role mapping file to understand how internal roles correspond to market benchmark categories. Then fetch the market benchmark data from the API endpoint and query the data warehouse for employee salary information grouped by department and role.

For each department, calculate the overall average internal salary, the corresponding market benchmark average across roles in that department, and the pay ratio which is the internal average divided by the market benchmark. Also determine what percentage of employees in each department earn above the market benchmark average and what percentage earn below it. For each individual role, compute the average internal salary, the market benchmark, the pay ratio, the dollar variance between internal and market, and classify the role as Above Market if the pay ratio exceeds 1.05, Below Market if it falls below 0.95, or At Market otherwise.

Create an Excel file called Salary_Benchmark.xlsx in the workspace with three sheets. The first sheet named Department Analysis should have columns Department, Avg_Salary, Market_Benchmark, Pay_Ratio, Above_Market_Count, Below_Market_Count, Above_Market_Pct, and Below_Market_Pct, with one row per department sorted alphabetically. The second sheet named Role Details should have columns Department, Role, Employee_Count, Avg_Salary, Market_Benchmark, Pay_Ratio, Variance_Amount, and Status, with one row per role sorted by department then role. The third sheet named Summary should have columns Metric and Value, listing Total_Employees, Overall_Avg_Salary rounded to two decimal places, Overall_Market_Avg, Overall_Pay_Ratio to four decimal places, Most_Above_Market_Role showing the role name with department in parentheses, Most_Above_Market_Ratio, Most_Below_Market_Role with department in parentheses, Most_Below_Market_Ratio, Total_Roles_Analyzed, Roles_Above_Market count where ratio exceeds 1.0, and Roles_Below_Market count where ratio is below 1.0.

Additionally, create a Word document called Compensation_Report.docx in the workspace. This report should contain an executive summary section describing the overall compensation positioning, a key findings section highlighting the most and least competitive positions, a department overview section summarizing each department's compensation status, and a recommendations section with specific actions based on the compensation policy guidelines regarding pay ratio thresholds.
