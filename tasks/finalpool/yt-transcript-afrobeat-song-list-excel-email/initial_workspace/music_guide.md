Afrobeat Mix Analysis Guide

This guide explains how to identify individual songs in an Afrobeat DJ mix from a video transcript.

Song Identification
In Afrobeat mixes, song titles and artist names typically appear together in the transcript text in one of these formats: SONG TITLE - ARTIST NAME, or ARTIST NAME - SONG TITLE. The DJ or lyric captions often include both the title and the performing artist separated by a dash or hyphen.

Transitions
Song transitions in a DJ mix happen roughly every 2 to 4 minutes. A typical Afrobeat mix of about 40 to 60 minutes in total will feature between 10 and 20 distinct tracks. When you see a new song title appear in the transcript, record its position as the start time.

Estimating Timing
The transcript provides approximate start times in seconds for each snippet. Use the first appearance of a song title or its distinctive lyrics to estimate the song start time. Calculate the duration as the difference between the start of that song and the start of the next song.

Output Format
Create each row in the Tracklist sheet with Track_Number starting from 1 and incrementing by 1 for each song in the order they appear in the mix. The Notes column should contain a brief one-phrase description such as the song style, energy level, or cultural significance.

Artist Summary
Group all tracks by artist name. For artists with multiple songs in the mix, sum up their total duration in seconds.
