Your company's strategic planning team requires a comprehensive competitive intelligence report to inform product development and pricing strategies. The first phase involves systematically reviewing competitor websites and publicly available pricing information. Using automated browsing tools, extract product specifications, feature lists, pricing data, and promotional offers from at least five major competitors in your market segment. Document all pricing tiers, bundling options, and special offers currently available.

In the second phase, consolidate all extracted data into a structured format within an analytical database. Create detailed product matrices showing how each competitor's offerings compare across key features, quality indicators, and pricing points. Include information about distribution channels, customer segments targeted, and any unique value propositions each competitor emphasizes in their marketing materials.

The third phase requires performing detailed comparative analysis on the collected data. Analyze pricing strategies to identify patterns such as premium positioning, value-based approaches, or competitive matching. Calculate price-to-feature ratios across product categories. Identify gaps in the competitive landscape where your company might differentiate or opportunity areas for competitive advantage. Assess market trends in product features and customer acquisition strategies.

In the fourth phase, create visualizations and summary tables in spreadsheet format showing your analysis. Develop charts comparing pricing across competitors, heat maps showing feature availability, and trend analyses. Create executive-friendly summaries that highlight key findings and opportunities. Prepare detailed appendices with all raw data and methodology documentation for reference.

The fifth phase involves drafting a comprehensive executive summary report documenting all findings. The report should include competitive landscape overview, pricing analysis, feature comparison, identified opportunities, and strategic recommendations for your company. Include risk assessments of competitive threats and suggestions for differentiation strategies. The sixth phase requires preparing the report in professional presentation format and sending it to executive leadership through email. Schedule a briefing meeting with strategy stakeholders to discuss findings and decide on next steps.
