You are a science educator preparing supplementary materials for a university study group. You want to build a quiz document based on recent episodes from the Veritasium science channel on the video platform, create a tracking spreadsheet in a cloud-based spreadsheet tool, schedule study sessions on the shared calendar, and notify your study group by email.

Your workflow should proceed as follows.

First, look up the Veritasium channel on the video platform. Retrieve its most recent videos, getting at least 8 to 10 of the newest ones. For each video, collect the title, description, duration in seconds, view count, and publication date. Based on the title and description, identify the primary scientific domain covered by each video, such as physics, mathematics, engineering, biology, or psychology.

Second, create a Word document named Veritasium_Science_Quiz.docx. Start the document with the heading "Veritasium Science Quiz - Q1 2026" at the top level. Then, for each video you retrieved, add a level-one heading using the video title or a short descriptive title of its topic. Under each heading, write three comprehension questions that a viewer would be able to answer after watching that video. The questions should relate to the scientific concepts covered. Assign a difficulty rating from 1 to 5 for each video section, where 1 is introductory and 5 is graduate-level, and note this rating after the heading. If any of the study session dates you schedule later conflict with existing calendar events, add a short note about the conflict at the end of the document.

Third, create a cloud spreadsheet titled "Veritasium Study Tracker". In the first sheet named Videos, include the following columns in order: Video_ID, Title, Topic, Duration_Min, Difficulty, Study_Status. Populate it with one row per video you retrieved, using the appropriate topic label and your difficulty rating. Set Study_Status to "Pending" for all videos initially. In a second sheet named Questions, include the following columns: Video_Title, Question_Number, Question_Text, Difficulty. Populate it with all the questions you wrote in the Word document, three rows per video.

Fourth, check the shared calendar for existing events in the next four weeks starting March 14 2026. Then create four weekly science study session events on the calendar, scheduled for Saturdays at 10:00 to 12:00, on March 14, March 21, March 28, and April 4 2026. Use the title "Science Study Session" for each event. Note any conflicts in the Word document as described above.

Fifth, send an email to studygroup@university.edu. The subject should reference the quiz document and study schedule. The body should briefly describe the topics covered, mention the four study sessions scheduled, and encourage members to review the cloud spreadsheet for the full video list and questions.

The study configuration file in your workspace is named quiz_format.json and provides the quiz title, the number of questions per video, and the difficulty scale. A guide document named study_config.md explains how to rate video difficulty and what topics to cover.

When all outputs are complete, claim the task as done.
