The HR department wants to understand the relationship between education levels and performance ratings across all departments in order to identify where training investments will have the greatest impact. There is a PDF file called Training_Policy.pdf in the workspace that describes the company's training budget allocation per education level. Please read it first to understand the policy context.

Then connect to the data warehouse and query employee data. Get the count and average performance rating grouped by department and education level, so that each department-education combination is represented. Sort the results alphabetically by department and then by education level.

Create an Excel file called HR_Education_Analysis.xlsx in the workspace with two sheets.

The first sheet should be called "Education Breakdown" with four columns: Department, Education_Level, Employee_Count, and Avg_Performance (rounded to 2 decimal places). Each row represents one unique department and education level combination, sorted alphabetically by Department first.

The second sheet should be called "Department Summary" with seven columns: Department (sorted alphabetically), Total_Employees, High_School_Count, Bachelor_Count, Masters_Count, PhD_Count, and Higher_Ed_Pct. Higher_Ed_Pct is the percentage of employees with Master's or PhD degrees out of the total for that department, rounded to 1 decimal place. The Diploma education level should be counted in total employees but not in any of the specific education level columns listed above.

After creating the Excel file, create a Google Form called "Training Interest Survey" with the following four questions: one asking for the respondent's department, one asking for their current education level, one multiple-choice question about their areas of training interest (with options such as Technical Skills, Leadership, Communication, and Data Analysis), and one asking about their preferred training format (with options like In-Person Workshop, Online Self-Paced, Live Virtual, and On-the-Job Coaching).

Finally, send an email to training@company.com with a subject summarizing the education analysis findings and a brief message body highlighting key insights about education distribution across departments.
