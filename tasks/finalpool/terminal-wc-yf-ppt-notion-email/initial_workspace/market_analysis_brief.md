# E-Commerce Market Positioning Strategy Brief

## Background
Our online store operates in the consumer electronics and home goods space, competing with major retailers. Understanding how broader economic conditions affect our sales performance is critical for strategic planning in Q2 2026.

## Key Strategic Questions

1. **Market Correlation**: How strongly does our monthly e-commerce revenue correlate with major market indices? If our revenue tracks consumer sentiment (AMZN), we should align pricing with market conditions. If it moves inversely, counter-cyclical strategies may be more effective.

2. **Category Vulnerability**: Which product categories are most affected by macroeconomic shifts? Categories with high price points may be more sensitive to market downturns.

3. **Competitive Positioning**: With Amazon (AMZN) as our primary competitor benchmark, how does their stock performance reflect consumer spending patterns that affect our business?

4. **Financial Sector Health**: JPM stock performance serves as a proxy for consumer credit availability and financial sector health, which can influence big-ticket purchases.

5. **Broad Market Health**: The Dow Jones Industrial Average (^DJI) provides an overall economic sentiment indicator.

## Analysis Period
- E-commerce data: All available order history (approximately March 2025 through February 2026)
- Market data: Full available price history for AMZN, JPM, and ^DJI

## Deliverables Expected
- Correlation analysis between store revenue and market indicators
- Category-level performance breakdown
- Strategic presentation for executive review
- Tracking system for strategic initiatives
- Executive summary and team communications
