You are a compensation analyst preparing a market-adjusted bonus review for the sales team. The compensation committee wants to understand whether current bonus payouts should be adjusted based on external market conditions before finalizing the Q4 bonus cycle.

Start by reading the compensation_policy.md file in your workspace. It explains the current bonus calculation methodology, which assigns bonuses based on the total revenue generated in each sales region. Each region's total revenue determines the bonus tier for all sales department employees assigned to that region. Also read the bonus_tiers.csv file which contains the exact tier thresholds and corresponding bonus percentages. Finally, read the budget_cap.json file which specifies the maximum total bonus pool the company has approved.

Query the data warehouse to get the total revenue and order count broken down by customer region from the orders and customers tables. You will also need to pull the list of employees in the Sales department from the HR employees table, including their name, salary, performance rating, years of experience, and role.

Write and run a Python script called compute_bonuses.py in your workspace. The script should determine each region's bonus tier based on the total regional revenue and the thresholds in bonus_tiers.csv. For each Sales department employee, compute their current bonus as their salary multiplied by the bonus percentage for their assigned region. Assign employees to regions in round-robin alphabetical order by employee name across the five regions sorted alphabetically (Asia Pacific, Europe, Latin America, Middle East, North America). Save the output as current_bonuses.json with each employee's name, region, salary, bonus percentage, and bonus amount.

Next, retrieve stock price data for the Dow Jones Industrial Average (ticker ^DJI), Amazon (AMZN), and ExxonMobil (XOM). For each, get the most recent closing price and the closing price from approximately one year earlier to compute the year-over-year percentage change.

Write and run a second Python script called market_adjustment.py. The script should compute a market adjustment factor based on the Dow Jones year-over-year change: if the DJI is up more than 10 percent year-over-year, the adjustment factor is 0.9 (reduce bonuses by 10 percent since the strong market already benefits compensation through other channels); if the DJI is down more than 10 percent, the factor is 1.1 (increase bonuses to retain talent in a weak market); otherwise the factor is 1.0. Apply this factor to every employee's current bonus to get their adjusted bonus. Also check if the total adjusted bonus pool exceeds the budget cap from budget_cap.json, and if so, proportionally scale all bonuses down to fit within the cap. Save the results as market_adjusted_bonuses.json.

Check the shared calendar for any existing events on the week of March 9 to March 13, 2026 between 9 AM and 5 PM Eastern Time. Find the first available 90-minute slot that does not conflict with existing events and schedule a new event called "Q4 Compensation Review Meeting" with the description "Review market-adjusted sales bonuses for Q4" at that time.

Create a cloud spreadsheet titled "Sales Compensation Analysis" with three sheets. The first sheet should be called "Rep_Performance" with columns Employee Name, Region, Revenue in Region, Order Count in Region, Salary, Performance Rating, Current Bonus. The second sheet should be called "Market_Adjustment" with columns Index or Ticker, Current Price, Year Ago Price, YoY Change Percent, Adjustment Factor. Include rows for ^DJI, AMZN, and XOM, but only the ^DJI row should have the adjustment factor filled in since the adjustment is based solely on the Dow Jones. The third sheet should be called "Adjusted_Bonuses" with columns Employee Name, Region, Current Bonus, Market Factor, Adjusted Bonus, Difference from Current.

Create a Word document called Compensation_Review_Memo.docx in your workspace. The memo should have the title "Q4 Compensation Review Memo" followed by sections for Background (explaining the purpose of the review), Methodology (describing the bonus calculation and market adjustment approach), Market Analysis (summarizing each stock's year-over-year performance and the resulting adjustment factor), Regional Performance Summary (listing each region's total revenue and order count), Budget Impact (comparing total current bonuses versus total adjusted bonuses and noting whether the budget cap was exceeded), and Recommendations (at least two recommendations for the committee regarding the bonus payouts).

Finally, write and run a validation script called validate_bonuses.py that reads market_adjusted_bonuses.json and confirms that no individual adjusted bonus exceeds 20 percent of the employee's salary, and that the total bonus pool is within the budget cap.
