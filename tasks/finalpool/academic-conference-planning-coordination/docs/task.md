Your academic conference organizing committee must coordinate all aspects of a major international conference to ensure excellent participant experience and successful scientific program. The first phase involves organizing the conference information and participant submissions. Gather all paper submissions received during the call for papers period. Create a comprehensive database of all submissions with author information, paper titles, abstracts, and subject areas. Document reviewer assignments and track which reviewers have been assigned to each paper. Set up systems to track reviewer acceptance and review completion status.

In the second phase, you will coordinate the peer review process with all assigned reviewers. Prepare and send detailed reviewer instructions and review forms to all appointed reviewers. Establish and communicate review deadlines and expectations. Monitor reviewer progress and send reminders to reviewers who have not completed assigned reviews. Aggregate all review scores and feedback for each submission. Calculate average scores and perform consensus analysis on reviewer ratings.

The third phase requires analyzing submissions and reviews to determine acceptance decisions. Calculate acceptance metrics showing overall acceptance rate and acceptance by topic area. Analyze reviewer comments to identify areas of strong consensus and areas of divergence. Make acceptance decisions ensuring appropriate balance across research areas and speaker diversity. Prepare notification communications for authors with decisions and feedback.

In the fourth phase, you will organize the conference program and schedule. Create a spreadsheet showing all accepted presentations with speaker information, presentation times, and session assignments. Optimize session scheduling to minimize conflicts and group related presentations into coherent thematic sessions. Arrange speaker presentation order within sessions and schedule breaks. Coordinate with speakers regarding presentation times and technical requirements.

The fifth phase involves preparing all attendee communications and conference logistics documentation. Create the conference program document showing complete schedule, speaker bios, paper abstracts, and venue information. Prepare registration instructions and logistics information for attendees including travel, accommodations, and parking. Generate orientation materials explaining conference navigation and participation options.

The sixth phase requires distributing information to all stakeholders and finalizing logistics. Send acceptance notifications and speaker instructions through email to all accepted authors. Create calendar invitations for all conference events and send to registered participants. Prepare final conference program for publication and distribution. Schedule coordination meetings with conference staff and volunteers to finalize all arrangements and ensure readiness.
