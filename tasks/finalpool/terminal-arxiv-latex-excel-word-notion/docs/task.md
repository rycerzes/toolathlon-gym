A research team is preparing a grant proposal on transformer architectures and needs a thorough literature review. The review criteria and proposal brief are in the workspace.

Search the paper archive for papers related to transformer models and attention mechanisms. For each paper found, retrieve its metadata (title, authors, publication year, categories) and also examine the LaTeX source to identify the methodology sections and key technical contributions.

Run text analysis scripts that extract and compare methodological approaches across papers. Specifically, identify the main method or architecture proposed in each paper and note any key innovations.

Create an Excel file called Literature_Review_Matrix.xlsx with three sheets. The first sheet "Paper_Catalog" should have columns ArXiv_ID, Title, Authors (first author's last name), Year, and Primary_Category, sorted by Year then ArXiv_ID. The second sheet "Methodology_Comparison" should have columns ArXiv_ID, Method_Name, Approach (a brief one-line description of the approach), and Key_Innovation (the main novel contribution), with one row per paper sorted by ArXiv_ID. The third sheet "Citation_Network" should have columns Citing_Paper (ArXiv_ID of the paper that cites), Cited_Paper (ArXiv_ID of the paper being cited), and Context (brief description of why cited), listing all citation relationships found between the papers in the archive.

Write a Word document called Literature_Review_Draft.docx that contains an introduction to the transformer architecture research landscape, a section comparing the methodologies of the retrieved papers, a discussion of how the papers relate to each other through their approaches, and a conclusion identifying gaps and future research directions.

Finally, create a knowledge base database called "Transformer Research Papers" with entries for each paper, including properties for the title, year, category, and a summary of the key contribution.
