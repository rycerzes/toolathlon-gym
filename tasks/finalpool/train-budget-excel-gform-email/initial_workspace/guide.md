# Budget Analysis Guide: Beijing to Shanghai Business Trip

## Overview
This guide explains how to structure the budget analysis for an 8-person Beijing to Shanghai business trip. The analysis covers three budget scenarios with distinct cost profiles.

## Budget Scenarios

### Budget Scenario
- Train class: Second class (cheapest available)
- Objective: Minimize total travel cost
- Use this scenario when cost control is the primary concern

### Standard Scenario
- Train class: First class on G1 high-speed train
- Objective: Balance comfort and cost
- G1 is the flagship Beijing-Shanghai express; first class provides extra legroom and a quieter cabin
- Use this scenario as the default recommendation

### Premium Scenario
- Train class: Business class (商务座)
- Objective: Maximum comfort for senior staff or client-facing roles
- Business class offers reclining seats, priority boarding, and complimentary meals
- Use this scenario for executives or when representing the company to clients

## Excel Sheet Structure

### Sheet: Travel_Options
Columns: Train_Number, Departure_Time, Arrival_Time, Duration_Minutes, Second_Class_Price, First_Class_Price, Business_Class_Price

### Sheet: Budget_Scenarios
Columns: Scenario, Train_Number, Class, Price_Per_Person, Num_Passengers, Total_Cost, Notes

### Sheet: Summary
Columns: Scenario, Total_Cost, Cost_Per_Person, Savings_vs_Premium, Recommendation

## Interpreting Budget Differences
- The difference between Budget and Standard scenarios shows the comfort premium per person
- The difference between Standard and Premium scenarios shows the executive upgrade cost
- Total trip savings = (Premium_Total - Budget_Total) across all 8 passengers
- Report should highlight the recommended scenario with justification based on trip purpose
