Assessment Psychometric Glossary

Difficulty Index refers to the proportion of students who answer a question correctly. It ranges from 0 to 1, where 0 means no student answered correctly and 1 means all students answered correctly. The acceptable range is typically 0.3 to 0.8. A difficulty index is computed at the quiz level as the average score divided by the maximum possible score (points possible multiplied by the number of questions).

Discrimination Index measures how well an item differentiates between high-performing and low-performing students. It is calculated as the difference between the proportion of correct responses in the top 27 percent of students and the bottom 27 percent. A minimum of 0.2 is expected for effective items.

Reliability refers to the consistency of an assessment instrument. Cronbach's alpha is the standard measure, with values above 0.7 considered acceptable for educational assessments.

Item Flagging occurs when a quiz's average difficulty falls outside the acceptable range of 0.3 to 0.8. Flagged items require review and potential revision before the next administration of the assessment.
