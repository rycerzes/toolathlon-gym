Skills Gap Assessment Methodology
===================================

Version 2.0 - Updated January 2026

Overview:
This document describes the standard methodology for assessing skills gaps across departments. The approach uses employee performance data as a proxy for skills proficiency.

Assessment Criteria:
- Performance ratings are measured on a scale of 1 to 5
- A department average performance rating below 3.21 indicates a skills gap in that department's core competency areas
- The Gap Score is calculated as: max(0, 3.21 - Department_Avg_Performance)
- Departments with Gap Score > 0 require immediate training intervention
- Departments with Gap Score = 0 should still be reviewed for continuous improvement

Priority Classification:
- High Priority: Gap Score > 0 (any non-zero gap indicates urgent training needs)
- Low Priority: Gap Score = 0 (department meets minimum proficiency threshold)

Recommended Actions:
- For High Priority departments: Identify relevant training courses and enroll at least 20% of staff
- For Low Priority departments: Encourage voluntary enrollment in advanced courses
- All recommendations should be communicated to department heads via email

Education Level Considerations:
- Track the distribution of education levels within each department
- Departments with higher proportions of PhD and Master's holders may need different training approaches than those with more High School or Diploma holders
- Tailor course recommendations based on education mix
