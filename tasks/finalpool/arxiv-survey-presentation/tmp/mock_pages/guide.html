Presenting Survey Results Effectively

Slide Structure

Begin with a title slide that clearly states the survey topic. Follow with a motivation slide explaining why this survey matters. Present the methodology for how papers were selected and compared.

Comparison Tables

Use comparison tables to show key metrics side by side. Include columns for method name, dataset, key metric, and main finding. Color-code cells to highlight best results.

Taxonomy Visualization

Present the taxonomy as a hierarchical diagram or structured list. Show how many papers fall into each category. This helps the audience quickly grasp the landscape.

Key Takeaways

End each section with a clear takeaway. The final slide should summarize the main findings and identify the most promising research directions. Include 2-3 specific open questions for future work.
