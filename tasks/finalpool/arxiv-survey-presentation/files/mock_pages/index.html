How to Structure a Literature Survey

Overview

A good literature survey systematically organizes existing research to identify trends, gaps, and future directions. The survey should go beyond simply listing papers and instead provide a structured analysis.

Comparison Criteria

When comparing papers, establish clear criteria such as methodology type, evaluation benchmarks, dataset size, model scale, and reported improvements. Use a consistent framework across all papers to enable fair comparison.

Taxonomy Development

Group papers into meaningful categories based on their primary approach. Common taxonomy dimensions include: the type of method (prompting, training, architecture), the target task (reasoning, generation, classification), and the scale of evaluation. A good taxonomy should be mutually exclusive and collectively exhaustive.

Writing Tips

Start with a clear research question. Organize papers chronologically or thematically. Highlight connections between papers. Identify open problems and future directions. Use tables and figures to summarize comparisons.
