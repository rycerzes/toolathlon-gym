Method Comparison and Tracking Guidelines

When evaluating methods from benchmark leaderboards, consider the following criteria for each method:

1. Performance Score: Record the exact score as reported on the leaderboard. Note the metric type (accuracy, F1, BLEU, etc.) and whether higher or lower is better.

2. Key Contribution: For each method with an associated paper, identify the single most important technical innovation. This should be a concise one-sentence summary of what makes this method different from prior work.

3. Dataset Coverage: Note which datasets were used in the evaluation. Methods evaluated on more diverse datasets tend to be more robust.

4. Reproducibility: Check if the paper provides code, model weights, or detailed hyperparameter settings.

5. Architecture Type: Classify the method by its underlying architecture (transformer, CNN, hybrid, etc.) to identify trends.

When comparing methods across tasks, look for cross-cutting patterns such as whether certain architectural choices consistently outperform others, or whether specific training strategies are more effective.

The tracker should be updated regularly as new results are published.
