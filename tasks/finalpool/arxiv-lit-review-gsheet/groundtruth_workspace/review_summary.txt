Prompt Engineering Literature Review - Summary of Findings

Papers Reviewed:
1. Chain-of-Thought Prompting Elicits Reasoning in Large Language Models (Wei et al., 2022)
2. Self-Consistency Improves Chain of Thought Reasoning in Language Models (Wang et al., 2022)
3. Automatic Chain of Thought Prompting in Large Language Models (Zhang et al., 2022)
4. Large Language Models are Zero-Shot Reasoners (Kojima et al., 2022)
5. Tree of Thoughts: Deliberate Problem Solving with Large Language Models (Yao et al., 2023)

Synthesis:
This literature review examines five seminal papers on prompt engineering techniques for improving reasoning in large language models. The foundational work on chain-of-thought prompting by Wei et al. demonstrated that providing step-by-step reasoning exemplars significantly enhances LLM performance on arithmetic, commonsense, and symbolic reasoning tasks. Building on this, self-consistency by Wang et al. improved results by sampling multiple reasoning paths and using majority voting, while Auto-CoT by Zhang et al. automated the demonstration construction process using clustering. Kojima et al. showed that even without few-shot examples, simply appending "Let's think step by step" enables effective zero-shot reasoning. Finally, Tree of Thoughts by Yao et al. generalized chain-of-thought prompting into a tree-based exploration framework with backtracking capabilities, enabling deliberate problem solving for tasks requiring strategic lookahead. Together, these papers trace the evolution of prompt engineering from manual few-shot chain-of-thought to automated, zero-shot, and structured exploration approaches for eliciting reasoning in large language models.
