You are the procurement manager for an e-commerce retail company. You need to plan a series of supplier visits to address stock shortages in your product catalog. Your task is to analyze the online store data to identify which suppliers need to be visited, plan the high-speed rail travel to the supplier cities, create a comprehensive visit plan in Excel, schedule the visits on the shared calendar, and send meeting invitations to the relevant suppliers.

Begin by querying the top-selling products from your online store to identify which product categories are generating the most revenue. For each of the top products, retrieve the detailed product information including the product name, stock keeping unit code, current stock level, and any supplier information available in the product metadata. Also check the current inventory situation to identify items that may be running critically low. Based on the sales volume and stock levels, identify the five most critical supplier relationships that warrant an in-person visit. Note that some suppliers are based in Shanghai and others are in Guangzhou.

Next, look up the station codes for Beijing, Shanghai, and Guangzhou. Query the available high-speed rail services departing from Beijing South Station to Shanghai Hongqiao Station on March 10, 2026. Also query departures from Beijing South Station to Guangzhou South Station on March 10, 2026. Identify the best train for each route, noting the train code, departure time, arrival time, and total journey duration in minutes. Look up the seat pricing for first class seats on both routes, as this is the company travel policy for procurement trips.

Create an Excel workbook named Supplier_Visit_Plan.xlsx. Add a sheet named Products with columns Product_ID, Name, SKU, Current_Stock, Monthly_Sales, Supplier_Name, Supplier_City, and Priority. Fill in at least five rows representing the critical suppliers you identified, assigning priority levels of High, Medium, or Low based on the stock criticality guidelines in the supplier visit guide. Add a second sheet named Travel_Plan with columns Supplier_City, Train_No, Departure_Time, Arrival_Time, Duration_Min, Seat_Class, and Ticket_Price_CNY. Add two rows covering the travel to Shanghai and the travel to Guangzhou. Add a third sheet named Visit_Schedule with columns Date, Supplier_Name, City, Meeting_Time, Purpose, and Status. Populate this with at least five visit appointments scheduled appropriately after the train arrival times, following the 30-minute buffer rule described in the guide.

Add two calendar events on March 10, 2026 to reflect the supplier visits. One event should represent the Shanghai supplier visit in the afternoon after the train arrives. A second event should mark the Guangzhou supplier meeting later that day.

Send an email to shanghai_supplier@techworld.com with a meeting request for March 10, specifying the proposed meeting time after you arrive in Shanghai. Send another email to gz_supplier@supplier.com with a similar meeting request for the Guangzhou visit. Finally send a summary email to procurement@company.com with an overview of the visit plan and reference to the Excel workbook.

Your workspace contains procurement_config.json which specifies the low stock threshold of 10 units, the selection criterion for the top 5 suppliers by sales volume, the travel date, the preferred seat class of first class, and your buyer email address. The file supplier_visit_guide.md explains the supplier prioritization logic, how to calculate buffer time after train arrival, the meeting schedule format, and priority scale definitions.
