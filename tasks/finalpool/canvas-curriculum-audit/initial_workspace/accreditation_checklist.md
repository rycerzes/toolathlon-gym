Accreditation Checklist Categories

The following categories are evaluated during the accreditation review process.

Structural Compliance checks whether each course meets the minimum requirements for assignments, quizzes, modules, and syllabus publication. Each category is scored as either compliant or non-compliant.

Content Quality evaluates whether the average scores on assessments fall within an appropriate range, indicating neither excessive difficulty nor insufficient rigor.

A course must pass all structural checks to be considered compliant. The overall compliance rate is calculated as the percentage of courses meeting all requirements.

Scoring system: Each requirement is binary (met or not met). A course with any unmet requirement is flagged as non-compliant, and the specific issues must be documented in the audit report.
