# Peer Benchmark Methodology

## Upside Potential Calculation

The upside potential for a stock is calculated as:

Upside_Pct = ((Target_Price - Current_Price) / Current_Price) * 100

A positive value indicates the stock is trading below its analyst target (potentially undervalued). A negative value indicates the stock is trading above its target (potentially overvalued).

## PE Relative Valuation

Each stock's trailing PE ratio is compared against its sector PE benchmark:

- If Trailing_PE > Sector_PE_Benchmark: the stock trades at a "Premium" to its peers
- If Trailing_PE <= Sector_PE_Benchmark: the stock trades at a "Discount" to its peers

## PE Premium/Discount Magnitude

The PE premium or discount magnitude is calculated as:

PE_Premium = Trailing_PE - Sector_PE_Benchmark

A positive value means the stock is more expensive relative to its sector average.
