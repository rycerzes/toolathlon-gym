You are a developer community organizer preparing to attend China Tech Expo 2026, a technology conference held at the Qufu International Convention Center from March 13 to March 14, 2026. Your task is to research relevant technology topics by finding the most popular videos from a well-known short-form tech channel, plan your train travel from Beijing to Qufu and back, organize all information into a structured spreadsheet, document the complete plan in your team knowledge base, and add all key events to the shared calendar.

Begin by searching for the top 5 most-viewed videos from the Fireship channel on the video platform, sorted by popularity. For each video, retrieve the full title, view count, approximate duration in minutes, and a brief description of the key technologies discussed. Identify the primary technology topic covered in each video so you can prepare relevant knowledge before the conference sessions.

Next, look up the train station codes for Beijing South station and Qufu East station using the train booking system. Query available high-speed trains departing Beijing South and arriving at Qufu East on March 12, 2026. Select the most suitable after-work evening departure train for the outbound journey. Then query return trains from Qufu East to Beijing South on March 15, 2026, and select a suitable afternoon return train. Retrieve the intermediate stop information for both selected trains.

Check the shared calendar to see any existing events between March 12 and March 15, 2026, and note any potential conflicts.

Create an Excel workbook named Tech_Conference_Plan.xlsx in your workspace. It must contain three sheets. The first sheet should be named Prep_Videos and contain columns: Rank, Title, Topic, View_Count, Duration_Min, and Key_Technologies. Populate it with the 5 videos you retrieved, ranked from most to least viewed. The second sheet should be named Travel_Details and contain columns: Leg, Train_No, From_Station, To_Station, Departure, Arrival, Duration_Min, Seat_Class, and Price_CNY. Add one row for the outbound journey and one row for the return journey, using the train codes and actual departure/arrival times you retrieved. Use first class for both legs and look up the actual price from the rail system for each leg. The third sheet should be named Conference_Schedule and contain columns: Date, Time, Activity, Location, and Notes. Add at least six rows covering departure on March 12, conference day one sessions and activities on March 13, conference day two sessions and networking on March 14, and the return journey on March 15.

Create a page in the team knowledge base titled "Tech Conference Preparation - Qufu March 2026". The page should include a travel details section describing both train journeys with the actual train numbers, departure and arrival times, and station names you retrieved. It should also include a Learning Resources section listing the five video topics you identified, with a brief note on why each technology topic is relevant to conference preparation.

Finally, add the following events to the shared calendar: a travel event titled "Conference Travel: Beijing to Qufu" on March 12 from 17:00 to 20:00; a full-day conference event titled "Tech Conference Day 1" on March 13 from 09:00 to 18:00; a second full-day conference event titled "Tech Conference Day 2" on March 14 from 09:00 to 18:00; and a return travel event titled "Return: Qufu to Beijing" on March 15 from 14:30 to 17:30.

Configuration details for this task are provided in the file conference_config.json and general preparation guidelines are in prep_guide.md, both located in your workspace.
