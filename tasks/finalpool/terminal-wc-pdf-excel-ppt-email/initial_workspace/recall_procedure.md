# Standard Operating Procedure: Product Recall Management

## Overview
This document outlines the standard process for handling product recalls from suppliers.

## Step 1: Impact Assessment (Day 1-2)
- Identify all affected products in the catalog
- Query order history to find all orders containing recalled products
- Compile list of affected customers with contact information
- Calculate financial exposure (revenue at risk)

## Step 2: Internal Communication (Day 2-3)
- Notify operations team with full impact summary
- Alert finance department with financial exposure details
- Brief legal team on customer communication plan
- Escalate to management if revenue at risk exceeds $5,000 (Major Recall)

## Step 3: Customer Notification (Day 3-5)
- Send personalized recall notices to all affected customers
- Provide clear instructions for product return
- Offer remediation options (refund, replacement, store credit)

## Step 4: Returns Processing (Day 5-14)
- Set up dedicated returns processing queue
- Track return rates and customer responses
- Coordinate with warehouse for returned product handling

## Step 5: Financial Reconciliation (Day 14-21)
- Process all refunds and credits
- Calculate actual financial impact
- Submit final report to supplier for reimbursement

## Classification Criteria
- **Major Recall**: Total revenue at risk exceeds $5,000
- **Minor Recall**: Total revenue at risk is $5,000 or below

## Reporting Requirements
- Excel impact assessment workbook with product, customer, financial, and timeline details
- Management briefing presentation
- Email notifications to operations, finance, and legal teams
