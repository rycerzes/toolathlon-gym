Music Blog Analysis Workflow Guide

This guide explains the editorial process for analyzing DJ mixes and music compilation videos for the AfroBeats Today blog.

Song Identification from Transcripts

When analyzing a DJ mix transcript, look for the following patterns to identify individual songs. Repeated lyric phrases appearing three or more times within a 30-second window indicate a song hook and mark the core of a track. Artist name mentions embedded in lyrics or as shoutouts indicate the performing artist. Abrupt changes in lyric style or topic indicate a song transition. Instrumental breaks of 10 seconds or more in the transcript text also signal transitions.

To calculate song duration, measure the time from the beginning of the first identifiable lyric of a song to the first lyric of the next song. If the transition is unclear, estimate 3 to 4 minutes as the average Afrobeats track length.

For the Afrobeats genre, common artists to look for include Burna Boy, Wizkid, Davido, Rema, CKay, Asake, Ayra Starr, Fireboy DML, and Tems. The main genre tags to use are Afrobeats for uptempo dance tracks, Afropop for melodic crossover tracks, Highlife for older West African influenced tracks, and Other for anything that does not fit the primary categories.

Publication Schedule

The blog publishes three article types per major music feature. The feature article runs 800 to 1200 words and covers the mix as a whole including track analysis and cultural context. The artist spotlight runs 400 to 600 words and focuses on one or two of the most prominent artists. The genre overview piece runs 600 to 900 words and places the music in broader genre context. Articles are published on Sundays at 10:00 in the morning for maximum engagement.

Data Organization

All song data should be organized in a spreadsheet with separate tabs for the tracklist, artist statistics, and publication plan. The team knowledge base entry should be created with an overview section and a linked song database for internal reference.
