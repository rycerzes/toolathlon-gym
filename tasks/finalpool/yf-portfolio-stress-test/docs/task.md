You are a risk manager responsible for stress-testing the firm's investment portfolio. Your workspace contains two files you must read first. The file Risk_Policy_2026.pdf describes the firm's risk management policy, including the maximum acceptable portfolio loss threshold of 20 percent and the Value-at-Risk limit at the 95th percentile of 15 percent. The file portfolio_weights.xlsx contains the current portfolio allocation across six positions: GOOGL at 25 percent, AMZN at 25 percent, JPM at 20 percent, JNJ at 15 percent, XOM at 10 percent, and gold futures (GC=F) at 5 percent.

After reviewing both files, fetch the stress test scenarios from the risk system at http://localhost:30224/api/stress_scenarios.json which provides four hypothetical scenarios. Each scenario specifies the percentage shock to equities, bonds, and gold, along with an estimated probability of occurrence.

Next connect to the stock data source and retrieve the current price for each of the six portfolio positions. You will use these prices to understand the current portfolio value and compute the dollar impact of each stress scenario.

Create an Excel workbook called Stress_Test.xlsx in your workspace with three sheets. The first sheet should be named "Scenario Analysis" with columns Scenario, Equity_Shock_Pct, Gold_Shock_Pct, Portfolio_Loss_Pct, Exceeds_Limit, and Probability_Pct. For each of the four scenarios, calculate the weighted portfolio loss by applying the equity shock to all equity positions (GOOGL, AMZN, JPM, JNJ, XOM which together represent 95 percent of the portfolio) and the gold shock to the gold position (GC=F at 5 percent). The portfolio loss is the sum of each position's weight multiplied by the applicable shock percentage. The Exceeds_Limit column should contain "Yes" if the absolute value of the portfolio loss exceeds 20 percent, or "No" otherwise.

The second sheet should be named "Position Impact" with columns Scenario, Symbol, Weight_Pct, Shock_Pct, and Position_Loss_Pct. This should have one row for each combination of scenario and position, resulting in 24 rows total (4 scenarios times 6 positions). The Position_Loss_Pct for each position is its weight times the shock percentage divided by 100, representing its contribution to total portfolio loss.

The third sheet should be named "Risk Summary" with columns Metric and Value. Include these rows: Worst_Case_Loss with the largest absolute portfolio loss percentage across all scenarios rounded to two decimal places, Scenarios_Exceeding_Limit with the count of scenarios where the portfolio loss exceeds the 20 percent maximum, Expected_Loss calculated as the probability-weighted average loss across all scenarios rounded to two decimal places (sum of each scenario's loss times its probability divided by 100), and Risk_Rating set to "Elevated" if any scenario exceeds the limit or "Acceptable" if none do.

Also create a PowerPoint presentation called Risk_Presentation.pptx in your workspace with at least five slides. The first slide should be a title slide with "Portfolio Stress Test Results" as the title. The second slide should summarize the portfolio composition and weights. The third slide should present the four stress scenarios and their portfolio impact. The fourth slide should highlight the worst-case scenario and whether it breaches the risk policy limit. The fifth slide should contain the risk summary metrics and a recommendation.
