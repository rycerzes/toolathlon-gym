You are the marketing manager for an online store and need to plan next quarter's coupon campaigns based on current coupon performance data.

First, connect to the WooCommerce store and retrieve all available coupon data including each coupon code, discount type, discount amount, and usage count.

Create an Excel file called Coupon_Campaign_Plan.xlsx in your workspace with two sheets. The first sheet should be called "Campaign Analysis" with columns: Code, Discount_Type, Amount, Usage_Count, Campaign_Priority. Assign Campaign_Priority based on usage count: coupons with more than 30 uses get "High" priority, coupons with 10 to 30 uses get "Medium" priority, and coupons with fewer than 10 uses get "Low" priority. Include all coupons you find.

The second sheet should be called "Next Quarter Plan" and should outline planned campaign launch dates for Q2 2026, with columns: Campaign_Name, Launch_Date, Expected_Reach, Notes.

Schedule the following campaign launch events in Google Calendar: a "HOLIDAY30 Campaign" on April 1, 2026 from 9:00 to 18:00, a "VIP20 Campaign" on May 1, 2026 from 9:00 to 18:00, a "SUMMER25 Campaign" on June 1, 2026 from 9:00 to 18:00, and a "FLASH50 Flash Sale" on June 15, 2026 from 9:00 to 18:00.

Create a Google Form titled "Coupon Campaign Feedback" to gather team input on campaign priorities. The form should include a multiple choice question asking about preferred discount type with options Percentage and Fixed Amount, a multiple choice question about best campaign timing with at least three options, and a short answer question for additional feedback.

Send an email to marketing@store.com with subject "Q2 2026 Coupon Campaign Plan" summarizing the coupon performance data and the planned campaigns for next quarter.
