The academic affairs office needs to identify students who are at risk of failing their courses and develop an intervention plan. A support resources page is available at http://localhost:30216/api/support_resources.json that lists the university's available academic support services including tutoring, study groups, and counseling. Retrieve this resource listing first to understand what support options exist.

Then pull student enrollment and grade data across all courses. A student is considered "at risk" if their current score is below 50 in any course. Students scoring below 40 should be classified as "Critical" risk level, while those between 40 and 50 are classified as "Warning" risk level. For each at-risk student, determine the most appropriate support resource based on the course subject area. Critical students should always receive a counseling referral in addition to subject-specific support.

Read the advisor contacts file in the workspace to identify the correct email recipients for the intervention report. Also review the intervention guidelines document which outlines the university's policies regarding early alert thresholds and mandatory meetings.

Create an Excel file called At_Risk_Report.xlsx with three sheets. The first sheet named "At Risk Students" should contain columns for Student_Name, Course, Current_Score, Risk_Level (either Critical or Warning), and Recommended_Support. Sort the rows by Current_Score from lowest to highest. The second sheet named "Course Summary" should have columns for Course, At_Risk_Count (number of at-risk students in that course), and Avg_Score (average current score across all enrolled students, not just at-risk ones). Include all 22 courses sorted alphabetically. The third sheet named "Action Plan" should contain columns for Priority, Action, Target_Group, Timeline, and Responsible, with at least five action items covering mandatory meetings for critical students, tutoring arrangements, study group creation, counseling referrals, and progress monitoring.

After creating the report, send an email to academic-support@uni.edu with the subject "At-Risk Student Intervention Report" summarizing the total number of at-risk students identified, the courses with the highest at-risk counts, and the recommended next steps.
