You are a wellness coach who believes that science education and healthy cooking should go hand in hand. Your task is to find health-related science education videos, match them with nutritious recipes, build a comprehensive wellness plan spreadsheet, write a wellness guide document, and schedule weekly wellness check-in sessions on the shared calendar.

Start by searching the video database for science videos from the Veritasium channel. Retrieve the full list of videos from the Veritasium channel sorted by newest first. From this list, select five videos and identify the science or wellness theme each one can be connected to. For example, a video about evolution connects to adaptation and resilience. A video about physics or materials connects to understanding the body and environment. A video about mathematics or cognition connects to brain health. Get the full details for each of the five selected videos including title, description, duration in minutes, and view count.

For each health topic identified from the videos, search the recipe knowledge base for matching healthy recipes. Look up recipes by category to find breakfast options, lunch options, and dinner options. Retrieve detailed information for at least three recipes per meal category, including the recipe name, ingredient list, cooking steps, estimated cooking time in minutes, and difficulty level. Based on the recipes found, design a seven-day meal plan that pairs the scientific health principles from the videos with practical nutritious meals.

Create a spreadsheet file named Wellness_Plan.xlsx with three sheets. The first sheet should be named Videos and have one row per video with columns Video_ID (the video identifier), Title (full video title), Health_Topic (the health or science topic the video covers), Duration_Min (video duration in minutes), and View_Count (total view count). This sheet must have at least four data rows. The second sheet should be named Recipes and have one row per recipe with columns Recipe_Name (the name of the recipe in its original language), Category (Breakfast, Lunch, or Dinner), Cook_Time_Min (cooking time in minutes as a whole number), Difficulty (Easy, Medium, or Hard), Key_Ingredient (the primary or most distinctive ingredient), and Health_Benefit (a brief phrase describing the nutritional or health benefit). This sheet must have at least eight data rows. The third sheet should be named Weekly_Meal_Plan with exactly seven rows (one per day of the week) and columns Day (Monday through Sunday), Breakfast (recipe name for breakfast), Lunch (recipe name for lunch), Dinner (recipe name for dinner), and Total_Recipes (always the value 3 since each day has three meals).

Create a Word document named Wellness_Guide.docx. Start with a main title heading "Science-Based Wellness Guide". Add a section with heading "Learning Resources" that lists each of the five Veritasium videos found, giving the video title and a one-sentence summary of what health concept it teaches. Add a section with heading "Recommended Recipes" that describes five standout recipes from the recipe search, giving the recipe name and a sentence about why it is nutritionally beneficial. Add a section with heading "7-Day Meal Plan" that presents the weekly meal plan in prose or list form, showing the breakfast, lunch, and dinner choice for each day of the week from Monday through Sunday.

Create four calendar events for Wednesdays in April 2026 (April 1, April 8, April 15, and April 22) from 07:00 to 07:30, each titled "Wellness Check-in". Include in each event description a note about reviewing meal plan adherence and reflecting on health topics from the video resources.
