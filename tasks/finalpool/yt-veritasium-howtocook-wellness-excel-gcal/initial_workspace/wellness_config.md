Science-Based Wellness Program Configuration Guide

Overview
This wellness program combines video-based science education with practical healthy cooking. The goal is to find health-related science videos and pair each health topic with appropriate nutritious recipes from a recipe knowledge base.

Selecting Health-Related Videos
Look for videos from the Veritasium channel that cover topics such as: nutrition and metabolism, the biology of exercise, sleep science, mental health and the brain, gut health and bacteria, immune system function, and the science of happiness and stress. Prioritize videos that have practical health implications viewers can act on.

Recipe Selection Criteria
For each health topic found in the videos, search the recipe knowledge base for recipes that align with the health principle. For example, a video about gut health would pair well with fermented foods or high-fiber vegetable dishes. A video about brain health might pair with omega-3 rich foods or colorful vegetables.

Meal Plan Format
The seven-day meal plan should cover Monday through Sunday. Each day has exactly three meals: Breakfast, Lunch, and Dinner. The Total_Recipes column should always be 3 since each day has three meals. Choose recipes from the Recipes sheet when building the plan.

Difficulty Scale
Use Easy for recipes with 5 or fewer steps or under 15 minutes cooking time. Use Medium for recipes with 6 to 10 steps or 15 to 30 minutes cooking time. Use Hard for recipes with more than 10 steps or over 30 minutes cooking time.

Calendar Check-ins
Schedule wellness check-in events on Wednesdays in April 2026. The first Wednesday is April 1, then April 8, April 15, and April 22. Each event runs from 07:00 to 07:30 and is titled Wellness Check-in. Use the description to note that the check-in covers meal plan review and health topic reflection.
