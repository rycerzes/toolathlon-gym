I manage our e-commerce operations and I need to put together an inventory health review with a repricing strategy based on current market conditions. This involves pulling data from a few different sources and consolidating it into one report.

First, check our online store for all products. I need every product's current stock level and the stock threshold that our team set (it is stored in each product's metadata fields along with supplier name and supplier contact email). Get the full product list including IDs, names, SKUs, categories, prices, stock quantities, and all of that metadata.

Second, I need the latest gold price from the financial data service. Use the symbol GC=F for gold futures. Get me the most recent closing price and also calculate the average closing price over the last 30 trading days. Gold prices are a leading indicator for our electronics supply chain costs so I want to track the trend.

Third, visit the supplier pricing portal at http://localhost:30148 to get the latest wholesale cost multipliers by product category. The page has a table showing each category and its updated cost multiplier. These multipliers tell us how much wholesale costs have changed, and we will use them to calculate suggested retail price adjustments.

Once you have all the data, create an Excel file called inventory_repricing_report.xlsx in the workspace with four sheets.

The first sheet should be called Inventory Status. It needs columns Product_ID, Product_Name (truncate names to 60 characters), SKU, Category, Current_Stock, Stock_Threshold, Stock_Gap (which is the threshold minus the current stock, but use 0 if stock is at or above the threshold), Status (use "Out of Stock" when stock is zero, "Critical" when stock is above zero but below the threshold, and "OK" when stock is at or above the threshold), Supplier_Name, and Supplier_Email. Sort the rows by Stock_Gap from highest to lowest, and for ties use Product_Name alphabetically ascending.

The second sheet should be called Repricing Strategy. It needs columns Product_ID, Product_Name (first 60 characters), Category, Current_Price, Cost_Multiplier (the value from the website for that product's category), Suggested_Price (which is Current_Price multiplied by the Cost_Multiplier, rounded to 2 decimal places), Price_Change (Suggested minus Current, rounded to 2 decimal places), and Change_Direction (write "Increase" if the change is positive, "Decrease" if negative, "No Change" if zero). Sort by Product_ID ascending.

The third sheet should be called Gold Impact. This is a simple two-column layout with labels in column A and values in column B. The rows should be Latest_Gold_Price (the close price on the most recent trading date, rounded to 2 decimal places), Gold_30Day_Avg (the average close price of the last 30 trading days, rounded to 2 decimal places), Gold_Trend (write "Rising" if the latest price is above the 30-day average, "Falling" if below, "Stable" if equal), Total_Products (total number of products in the store), Out_Of_Stock_Count (number of products with zero stock), Critical_Stock_Count (number of products with stock above zero but below threshold), and Categories_With_Price_Increase (count of categories where the cost multiplier is greater than 1.0).

The fourth sheet should be called Summary. Also a two-column layout. The rows should be Products_Needing_Restock (count of products where stock is below the threshold), Avg_Stock_Gap (average of the Stock_Gap values for products where the gap is greater than zero, rounded to 1 decimal place), Total_Price_Increases (count of products that would see a price increase), Total_Price_Decreases (count of products that would see a price decrease), and Avg_Price_Change_Pct (average of Price_Change divided by Current_Price times 100 across all products, rounded to 2 decimal places).
