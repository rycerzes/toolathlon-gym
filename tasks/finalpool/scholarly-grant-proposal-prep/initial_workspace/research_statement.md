Principal Investigator Research Statement

Dr. Elena Rodriguez, Associate Professor of Computer Science

My research program focuses on the development of trustworthy and robust artificial intelligence systems, with particular emphasis on machine learning safety and interpretability. Over the past five years, my lab has produced significant contributions in adversarial robustness, explainable AI methods, and the application of AI to healthcare challenges.

Our core research areas include developing interpretable deep learning architectures that provide meaningful explanations for their predictions, creating robust training methods that maintain performance under distribution shift and adversarial attack, and applying machine learning to medical imaging for early disease detection.

We have strong expertise in neural network analysis, formal verification of ML systems, and clinical AI deployment. Our recent work on attention-based interpretability methods has been widely adopted in the medical imaging community, and our adversarial training framework has been used by several industry partners.

Looking forward, I am particularly interested in pursuing research on AI safety for large language models, robust medical imaging systems for resource-limited settings, and interpretable drug discovery pipelines using graph neural networks.

Key publications: 15 papers in top venues (NeurIPS, ICML, AAAI), h-index 28, total citations 3,200.
