Academic affairs wants to conduct a grade equity review to see whether grading standards have remained consistent across consecutive fall semesters. We need to look at courses that were offered in both Fall 2013 and Fall 2014 and compare how students performed in each offering. There is a grading standards PDF in your workspace called Grading_Standards.pdf that defines the grade bands and equity thresholds we use, along with a guide.md that explains the methodology.

Pull course listings and student grade data from the learning management system. Focus on fall semester offerings from 2013 and 2014. For each course that appears in both years, compute the average student score and pass rate for each year. A student's score should be their average across all assignment submissions in that course.

Create an Excel file called Grade_Equity_Analysis.xlsx with three sheets. The first sheet should be called Course Comparison and should have columns Course_Name (the base course name without the semester, sorted alphabetically), Fall_2013_Mean (rounded to 1 decimal), Fall_2014_Mean (rounded to 1 decimal), Score_Difference (the 2014 mean minus the 2013 mean, rounded to 1 decimal), Fall_2013_Pass_Rate (rounded to 1 decimal), Fall_2014_Pass_Rate (rounded to 1 decimal), Pass_Rate_Change (rounded to 1 decimal), and Equity_Status. The equity status should be Acceptable if the absolute score difference is less than 5, Concerning if between 5 and 10 inclusive, or Action Required if greater than 10. Only include courses that have data in both fall semesters.

The second sheet should be called Grade Distribution and should have columns Course_Name, Year, A_Count, B_Count, C_Count, D_Count, F_Count, and Total_Students. There should be two rows per course, one for 2013 and one for 2014. Use the grade bands from the standards document to classify students.

The third sheet should be called Summary with columns Metric and Value. Include rows for Total_Courses_Compared, Courses_Acceptable, Courses_Concerning, Courses_Action_Required, Overall_Avg_2013 (rounded to 1 decimal), Overall_Avg_2014 (rounded to 1 decimal), and Overall_Change (rounded to 1 decimal).

Write a Word document called Equity_Report.docx with sections for Executive Summary, Methodology, Course-by-Course Analysis, Recommendations, and Appendix. The executive summary should highlight key findings. The course-by-course section should discuss each compared course. The recommendations section should call out any courses requiring faculty review.

For each course that has an Action Required equity status, schedule a 45-minute faculty review meeting on Google Calendar during the week of March 16 to 20, 2026. Title each meeting with the course name followed by Grade Equity Review and include the equity details in the description.
