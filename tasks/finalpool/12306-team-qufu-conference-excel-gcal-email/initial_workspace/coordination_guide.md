This guide explains how to coordinate multi-city academic travel for conference attendance.

Synchronized Arrival Policy:
All team members should arrive at the destination within 30 minutes of each other to enable a coordinated ground transfer from the station to the conference venue. An arrival time difference of 30 minutes or less is considered acceptable.

Train Selection Process:
Step 1: Query available trains from each departure city to the destination on the travel date.
Step 2: For each city, identify the train with the best arrival time at the destination.
Step 3: Compare the arrival times of the selected trains from different cities.
Step 4: If the difference is 30 minutes or less, the coordination target is met.
Step 5: If the difference exceeds 30 minutes, consider alternative trains.

Spreadsheet Documentation:
The outbound travel plan should be recorded in a sheet named Outbound with one row per team member.
The return travel plan should be recorded in a sheet named Return with one row per team member.
A Coordination_Notes sheet should document key logistics checkpoints.

Email Notification Format:
Send separate emails to each team group. Include the train number, departure station, departure time, arrival station, arrival time, and ticket price. Remind team members to arrive at the departure station at least 30 minutes before departure.

Return Travel:
Book return trains for all team members on the final conference day. Beijing team members return to Beijing South and Shanghai team members return to Shanghai Hongqiao.
