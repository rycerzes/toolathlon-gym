HR needs to prepare a comprehensive diversity and inclusion report for the upcoming board meeting. Industry benchmark data is published on an internal diversity benchmark portal at http://localhost:30211/ which includes an overview page with overall industry averages for education level distribution, age group distribution, and marital status distribution, as well as a details page with department-type-specific benchmarks. You need to visit this portal to collect the benchmark data, then query the HR data warehouse to get our actual workforce demographics and compare them against the industry standards.

Begin by reading the D&I framework document in the workspace to understand the company's strategic goals and the presentation outline to understand the expected slide structure. Then navigate to the benchmark portal, visiting both the main page and the details page to collect all benchmark percentages. Query the data warehouse for employee demographics including education level, age, marital status, job satisfaction, work-life balance, and years of experience, grouped by department.

For the education analysis, calculate the percentage of employees at each education level both overall and by department, and compare against the overall industry benchmarks from the portal's main page. For the age analysis, group employees into Under 30, 30 to 39, 40 to 49, and 50 plus categories, calculate percentages by department, and compare against the benchmark. For marital status, calculate the overall distribution and compare against benchmarks. Classify each metric as Aligned if the gap is within 3 percentage points, Above if internal exceeds benchmark by more than 3 points, or Below if internal falls short by more than 3 points.

Create an Excel file called Diversity_Analysis.xlsx in the workspace with four sheets. The first sheet named Education Distribution should have columns Department, Education_Level, Count, Internal_Pct, Benchmark_Pct using the overall benchmarks from the portal main page, and Gap, sorted by department then education level. The second sheet named Age Distribution should have columns Department, Age_Group, Count, Internal_Pct, Benchmark_Pct, and Gap, sorted by department then age group. The third sheet named Overall Scorecard should have columns Metric being either Education or Age or Marital Status, Category being the specific level or group, Internal_Value as the overall company percentage, Benchmark_Value, Gap, and Status being Aligned or Above or Below based on the 3 point threshold. Include all education levels, all age groups, and both marital status categories. The fourth sheet named Summary should have Metric and Value columns listing Total_Employees, Total_Departments, Average_Age, Average_Experience_Years, Average_Job_Satisfaction, Average_Work_Life_Balance, and Education_Levels count of distinct education levels.

Also create a PowerPoint presentation called DEI_Board_Report.pptx in the workspace with at least five slides. Include a title slide, an executive summary slide with key workforce statistics, a slide on education distribution comparing internal versus benchmark data, a slide on age distribution comparison, and a recommendations slide with at least four specific action items for improving diversity alignment.
