Your sales organization must conduct a comprehensive audit of territory performance to optimize sales force allocation and identify opportunities for revenue growth. The first phase involves extracting complete sales data from your business management system. Gather all sales transactions for the past two years including deal size, close date, customer location, and sales representative. Extract customer account information including industry, company size, location, current revenue, and growth trajectory. Document all opportunities in the pipeline with status, estimated close date, and expected revenue.

In the second phase, you will analyze sales performance by geographic territory. Calculate total revenue, win rates, average deal size, and sales cycle length for each territory. Identify territory maturity by analyzing account age and growth patterns. Map customer distribution across territories and identify geographic areas with low penetration or high growth potential. Calculate coverage ratios showing sales representative workload and territory size by various metrics.

The third phase requires performing detailed analysis to identify underperforming regions and sales gaps. Compare actual performance against historical benchmarks, industry standards, and top-performing territories. Identify territories where revenue is flat or declining despite market growth. Analyze reasons for underperformance including sales representative factors, market saturation, competitive pressure, or territory design issues. Identify adjacent markets and customer segments currently underserved.

In the fourth phase, you will create comprehensive performance dashboards and KPI tracking systems. Design visualizations showing territory performance metrics, pipeline health, win rates, and revenue trends. Create dashboards for sales management oversight showing individual representative performance, territory performance, and organizational trends. Develop performance scorecards comparing territories against key metrics and improvement targets.

The fifth phase involves conducting strategic analysis and developing recommendations. Analyze whether territory realignment could improve efficiency and coverage. Evaluate opportunities for market expansion into underserved geographies. Identify high-potential customer segments for targeted development. Develop coaching and support recommendations for underperforming territories.

The sixth phase requires preparing a comprehensive audit report with strategic recommendations for sales leadership. Document findings about current territory performance, identified gaps, and recommended changes. Prepare implementation plans for territory realignment or market expansion including timelines and resource requirements. Send the report to senior management through email and schedule a strategic review meeting to discuss recommendations.
