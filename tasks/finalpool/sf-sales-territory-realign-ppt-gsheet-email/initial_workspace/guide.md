# Territory Analysis Methodology

## Revenue Per Customer
Revenue Per Customer = Total Revenue / Customer Count

This metric indicates how effectively each territory is monetizing its customer base. A higher value suggests strong upselling or larger deal sizes.

## Customer Concentration Index (HHI)
The Herfindahl-Hirschman Index measures revenue concentration across segments within a territory.

HHI = Sum of (Segment Revenue Share)^2

Where Segment Revenue Share = Segment Revenue / Total Territory Revenue

Interpretation:
- HHI < 0.15: Well-diversified territory
- HHI 0.15-0.25: Moderate concentration
- HHI > 0.25: High concentration risk

## Revenue Share Percentage
Revenue Share Pct = (Region Revenue / Total Company Revenue) * 100

This shows each territory's contribution to total company revenue.

## Variance Analysis
Variance = Actual Revenue - Target Revenue

A negative variance indicates underperformance against the target.

## Priority Classification
Based on the gap between actual and target revenue:
- High Priority: Gap exceeds $50,000 below target
- Medium Priority: Gap between $25,000 and $50,000 below target
- Low Priority: Revenue at or above target, or gap less than $25,000

## Territory Health Assessment
A territory is considered healthy when:
1. Revenue per customer exceeds $1,400
2. No single segment accounts for more than 35% of territory revenue
3. Revenue share is between 15% and 30% of total company revenue
4. Actual revenue meets or exceeds the assigned target
