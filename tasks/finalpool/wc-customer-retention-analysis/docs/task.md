We need to conduct a customer retention analysis for our e-commerce store. The goal is to identify customers who may be at risk of churning and take proactive steps to retain our most valuable ones. Our industry benchmarks API is available at http://localhost:30206/api/benchmarks.json and provides key metrics we should compare against.

Start by reading the retention_strategy.md file in the workspace which describes our customer segmentation rules and retention approach. Also read the email_template.txt file which contains the template we use for retention outreach emails. Review the customer_tiers.xlsx file which shows the spending thresholds for each tier, and the outreach_history.csv which logs previous retention campaigns.

Next, fetch the industry benchmarks from the API endpoint at http://localhost:30206/api/benchmarks.json. This will give you the average repeat purchase rate, average order frequency, average lifetime value, and the churn threshold in days. The churn threshold defines how many days of inactivity indicate a customer is at risk.

Then pull all customer data from our e-commerce platform, including each customer's total spending, number of orders, and order history. For each customer who has made at least one purchase, determine their most recent order date and calculate how many days it has been since that order (use March 7, 2026 as the reference date). Classify each customer into a segment based on their total spending: customers who have spent more than $500 are "VIP", those who spent between $100 and $500 (inclusive of $100) are "Regular", and those who spent less than $100 are "New". Mark a customer as "at risk" if the number of days since their last order exceeds the churn threshold of 90 days from the benchmarks.

Create an Excel workbook called Customer_Retention.xlsx in the workspace with three sheets. The first sheet should be named "Customer Analysis" with columns: Customer_Name (first and last name), Email, Total_Spent (rounded to 2 decimal places), Order_Count, Last_Order_Date (formatted as YYYY-MM-DD), Days_Since_Last (integer number of days since last order), Segment (VIP, Regular, or New), and At_Risk (Yes or No). Include all customers who have made at least one purchase. Sort by Total_Spent descending.

The second sheet should be named "Retention Metrics" with two columns: Metric and Value. Include these rows: Total_Active_Customers (count of customers with at least one order), VIP_Count, Regular_Count, New_Count, At_Risk_Total (total number of at-risk customers across all segments), At_Risk_VIP_Count (number of VIP customers who are at risk), Store_Repeat_Rate (percentage of customers with more than one order, rounded to 1 decimal place), Benchmark_Repeat_Rate (from the API, 35.0), Store_Avg_LTV (average total_spent across all customers with purchases, rounded to 2 decimal places), and Benchmark_Avg_LTV (from the API, 250.0).

The third sheet should be named "Action Plan" with columns: Customer_Name, Email, Segment, Days_Inactive, Recommended_Action. Include only VIP customers who are at risk, sorted by Days_Inactive descending. The Recommended_Action for each should be "Send personalized retention email".

Send a personalized retention email to each at-risk VIP customer. Use the email template from the workspace, replacing the placeholders with the customer's first name and their days of inactivity. Send each email from retention@store.com to the customer's email address. The subject line should be "We miss you, [FirstName]!" where [FirstName] is the customer's first name.

Finally, create a Google Sheet titled "Retention Outreach Log" with a sheet called "Outreach" containing columns: Date (use 2026-03-07 for all entries), Customer_Name, Email, Segment, Days_Inactive, and Action_Taken (use "Retention email sent" for each). Include one row for each at-risk VIP customer that was emailed.
